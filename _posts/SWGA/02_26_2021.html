STATEWIDE

  1. Gov. Brian Kemp announced the next expansion of eligibility criteria for the COVID-19 vaccine at an update on Thursday. Pre-K through 12 educators and school staff, adults with intellectual and developmental disabilities and their caregivers, and parents of children with complex medical conditions will be eligible beginning Mar 8. Others with severe underlying health conditions are not yet eligible. Read more.

    Related: The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention launched a new search tool for finding C-19 vaccines using your zip code and preferred search radius. The list is not comprehensive and lacks mass-vaccination and public sites but indicates whether specific locations have vaccines in stock or not. As of Friday morning, there are 38 providers within 50 miles of Bainbridge, but Rx Drug Center is the only location listed as currently having vaccines in stock in Decatur Co. Search on vaccinefinder.org.

    Good news: Nursing and long-term care homes across the state saw a sharp decline in new C-19 cases and deaths this month. Read more.

SWGA

  1. The Grady Co Sherrif’s Office and the GA Historical Society are investigating the vandalism of The Birthplace of Jackie Robinson historical marker in Cairo. Unknown parties shot at the sign, leaving multiple bullet holes on both sides. Due to the marker’s remote location, authorities are unsure when the vandalism took place and have yet to identify any suspects. Read more.

  2. The Southwest GA Regional Library system will open doors for in-person services starting March 1. One-at-a-time, one-hour appointments are available; face coverings are required to enter. Curbside service continues at all locations. Curbside hours and appointment times vary by location. Get the details.

Spotlight

This week is National Invasive Species Awareness Week, a nationwide effort to spread awareness of invasive plant species and their impact. Introduced species can crowd out native species and disrupt local ecosystems. Several frequently-encountered garden plants in our area are considered invasive, such as nandina, Mexican petunia, Japanese honeysuckle, and the golden rain tree.

Learn more about the GA Invasive Species Task Force species of concern or check out the University of FL Center for Aquatic and Invasive Plants plant directory for species identification, removal strategies, and suggested native replacements.

More: The GA Native Plant Society Annual Symposium is this weekend. The event is virtual this year and opens to the public for $10 a day. This year’s focus is on the critical relationships between GA native plants and GA native wildlife. Register here.
