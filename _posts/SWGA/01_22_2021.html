STATEWIDE

  1. GA pushes to vaccinate more as cases and deaths spike, and 5 cases of the new, more contagious COVID-19 variant are confirmed in the state. Gov. Kemp stated in a press conference Thursday that state hospitals cannot handle another surge in C-19 cases. Dr. Toomey of the GA Dept of Public Health says demand for vaccines will continue to outstrip supply until the federal government increases allotments to the states. She encouraged residents to “go where you can, where there’s availability” - there are no county residency restrictions for vaccination. Read more.

  2. K-12 public school teachers and staff in GA will receive a one-time $1K bonus. The payments will go to teachers, paraprofessionals, counselors, nurses, custodians, bus drivers, nutrition staff, clerical staff, and others who work at GA public schools. Funds will come from the GA Department of Education’s reserve portion of CARES 2 stimulus funds and the Governor’s Office. Read more.

SWGA

  1. A fatal two-car collision occurred at the intersection of GA 310 and GA 38 earlier this month. Pearl Stubbs, age 63, passed through the intersection on Jan 11 when her vehicle was struck by a Ford Econoline transit bus. She was later pronounced dead at Memorial Hospital and Manor. Pearl retired from West Bainbridge Elementary last school year after 25 years of service to the Decatur Co. Board of Education. Read more.
