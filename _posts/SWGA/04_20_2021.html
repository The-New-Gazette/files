SWGA

  1. A team from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention spent a week in SWGA to learn more about vaccination reservations. The group met with residents of Dougherty County to better understand vaccine reluctance and how to encourage more people to take the vaccine. Findings include people’s desire for more information about the vaccines and the need to increase accessibility by providing transportation or taking vaccines to trusted locations via pop-up clinics. Another team spent the week in Bacon County, GA. Read more.

DECATUR CO.

  1. GA Division of Family & Children Services (DFCS) representatives provided an update on cases and statistics in Decatur County at a Kiwanis Club meeting last week. In 2019, there were 110 severe cases of abuse or neglect in the county; this number rose to 246 in 2020. There are currently 49 children in foster care, and 99 cases of abuse or neglect have been reported this year. To report child abuse, call 1-855-422-4453. Read more.
