IMPORTANT

  1. The CDC is urging Americans to avoid travel and large family gatherings for Thanksgiving this year. To help slow the spread of COVID-19, the agency recommends limiting in-person celebrations to those within your immediate household. Although the increase in C-19 hospitalizations has been slower in GA than in some other parts of the country, 21 GA counties are currently at “tipping point” status. Read more.

TRI-STATE AREA

  1. FL Highway Patrol found a deceased man near Marianna, FL, on Wed. who appears to be the victim of a dog attack. The victim’s injuries and tracks found at the scene indicate the man may have been attacked by a pack of stray dogs roaming the area. Jackson Co. Sheriff’s office and Animal Control will be setting traps and encourage caution in the area of Kirkland Rd. Read more.

  2. United Airlines is now serving the Tallahassee airport. The airline has added daily direct flights to George Bush Intercontinental/Houston Airport, operated by SkyWest Airlines. Read more.

  3. The Chattahoochee River is one of the water bodies highlighted in this year’s Dirty Dozen Report. The annual report by the Georgia Water Coalition highlights policy and pollution threats to GA water. The largest threat right now to the Chattahoochee comes from unchecked sewage discharge. Read more.
