News

SWGA

  1. Everyone aged 16 and older is now eligible for the C-19 vaccine in GA. Governor Kemp announced the expanded eligibility on Tuesday and urged Georgians to book appointments as soon as possible. In response to vaccine hesitancy in rural and southern areas of the state, officials have rerouted 70% of this week’s vaccine allotment to sites in metro areas with higher demand. Read more.

    More

    • Vaccines are free for all residents, and appointments can be scheduled at SWGA Public Health through the call center at 229-352-6567, or you can find a vaccination site here.
    • The SWGA district does not offer the Pfizer vaccine, the only vaccine currently approved for teens under 18. You can search for providers by vaccine type here.

  1. Ameris Bank donated $2M to 19 rural hospitals in GA as part of the GA HEART Hospital Program. The program partners with the GA Rural Hospital Tax Credit bill to increase funding for hospitals in rural areas by providing income tax credits for contributions to qualified rural hospitals. In SWGA, Archbold Memorial Hospital, Colquitt Regional Medical Center, Donalsonville Hospital, and Miller County Hospital will receive donations. Read more.

    More: Learn more about the GA HEART program here.
