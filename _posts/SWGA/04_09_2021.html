News

SWGA

  1. Multiple SWGA sheriffs added their signature to a letter to the President asking him to reverse course on current immigration policies and resume construction on the border wall. Sheriffs in Cook, Decatur, Lanier, Miller, Thomas, Tift, and Worth counties signed the letter which accuses the current administration of “sanctioning lawlessness” and requests a return to the border policies of the previous administration. Read more or read the letter.

  2. Damage from Hurricane Michael continues to impact south GA. The remaining debris from the storm is providing fuel for wildfires - there have been five in Dougherty and Lee counties in recent weeks. The GA Forestry Commission encourages people to continue the cleanup and make sure to get a permit and keep a water hose handy when burning yard debris. Read more.

CITY OF BAINBRIDGE

  1. Construction and traffic updates:
  • Resurfacing of Calhoun, Broad, and Shotwell Streets in Bainbridge begins next week. You can subscribe to project and traffic pattern updates by texting DOTPAVING to 888-777.
  • Damaged sewer lines are under repair in the Lake Douglas area near College Road - the college entrance and a portion of Thomas Drive are blocked.
  • College Street is open to thru traffic but will be closed for resurfacing next week, weather pending.
