STATEWIDE

  1. Five additional mass vaccination sites opened across GA this week as more Georgians became eligible to receive the COVID-19 vaccine. The state-operated locations currently only administer the Pfizer vaccine and are available by appointment only. You can pre-register for an appointment and find current eligibility criteria here. View the complete list of locations here.

    Locally: The Southwest Public Health District has received doses of the Johnson & Johnson C-19 vaccine for distribution amongst all 14 SWGA Health Department locations. Appointments are required. Read more.

SWGA

  1. Phoebe Emergency Center and Abraham Baldwin Agricultural College (ABAC) will partner to address the nursing shortage in SWGA by providing additional funding, clinical rotations, and training opportunities. The partners will admit 40 additional students into the nursing program and focus on recruiting students in the Tifton and Bainbridge areas. Read more.

DECATUR COUNTY

  1. Decatur County voters opted to renew the Education Special Purpose Local Option Sales Tax (ESPLOST) and Issuance of Bonds in this week’s election. The penny sales tax helps fund renovations and improvements for the school district. It must be renewed by voters every five years. Turnout for the election was 4.18%, with 550 voting in favor of renewal and 166 opposed. Read more.

    Related: Seminole County School officials have proposed constructing a new facility for pre-K through twelfth grade instead of continuing with costly repairs to existing facilities. The new facility would be partially funded through ESPLOST. Read more.
