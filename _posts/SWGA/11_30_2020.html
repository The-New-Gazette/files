SWGA

  1. Temps are expected to drop below freezing tonight and tomorrow night. Daytime temps will remain in the 50s through Wed. See the temps and check the forecast.

  2. Georgia growers are expected to produce up to 120 million pounds of pecans this year, but profits remain low. This is the best crop in quality and quantity since Hurricane Michael in 2018, which heavily damaged orchards across SWGA. Trade tensions with China and cheap nuts from other countries have kept prices low, making this the third hard year in a row for area growers. Read more.

    Extra: UGA researchers are working on a free mathematical model for GA pecan growers and processors to predict how postharvest storage and handling techniques will affect pecan quality. Read more.

  3. An Augusta University study found that 1 in 5 tested residents of Dougherty County had C-19 antibodies in September. The area was targeted for antibody study after a surge in C-19 cases and deaths this Spring. 613 residents participated in the study and 132 tested positive. Read more.

    Related: The GA Department of Public Health is still not including positive C-19 results from rapid antigen tests in the statewide or county level charts and maps on the daily dashboard. Read more.
