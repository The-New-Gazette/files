WEATHER

  1. The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration released its forecast Thursday for the 2021 Atlantic hurricane season, which runs from June - November. Meteorologists expect to see 13 to 20 named storms, with six to ten becoming hurricanes and three to five of those escalating to major hurricanes with winds over 110 mph. Typical seasons of the last three decades have had 14 named storms, seven hurricanes, and three major hurricanes annually. Read more.

  2. Hot Summer temperatures arrive across the South next week. In SWGA, highs are expected to run between 98° - 102° on Monday - Friday, with little chance of rain. The week of June 10 - 15, 2011 was the last time our area had a week’s worth of 100° and up. Read more.

    More: Southwest Georgia Public Health and the National Weather Service encourage people to stay safe in the heatwave. Check on elderly neighbors and relatives, provide water and shade for outdoor animals, drink plenty of water, and do not leave pets or people in hot cars. Read more.

DECATUR COUNTY

  1. Three men attempted to steal a stand-alone ATM from Regions Bank last Saturday morning. Bainbridge Public Safety arrived at the scene to find the suspects parked near the machine with ropes and chains connecting a truck to the ATM. The case is under active investigation; those with information can contact BPS at 229-248-2038. Read more.

  2. Recently convicted inmate Enrique Martinez was found dead in his Decatur County jail cell last Friday. Martinez was sentenced to life last Wednesday by the Decatur County Superior Court after his conviction on rape and child molestation charges. Housed within the general population at the jail, Martinez was found with a note expressing apologies and remorse for his crimes. Read more.
