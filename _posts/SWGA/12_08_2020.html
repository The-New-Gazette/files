TRI-STATE AREA

  1. The Tallahassee home of former FL Department of Health (DOH) scientist Rebekah Jones was raided yesterday by FL state police. Jones crowdsourced and launched an independent COVID-19 dashboard earlier this year after she was fired from DOH. Law enforcement raided her home to seize computer equipment after DOH filed a complaint regarding unauthorized access to a department messaging system. Read more.

    More: Jones created Florida COVID Action to provide what she claims is a more accurate picture of the C-19 case count in FL. She has since also created The COVID Monitor to help keep track of cases in school districts across the USA. Read more.

SWGA

  1. Department of Public Health (DPH) doctors have seen patients in SWGA with both flu and COVID-19 at the same time. Flu cases have been found in Dougherty, Thomas, Mitchell, and Colquitt counties. At least three cases tested positive for both flu and C-19. Flu shots are available at local health departments at low or no cost for those without insurance. Read more.

  2. Advanced voting for Seminole Co. begins Mon, Dec 14, at 200 S. Knox Ave. in downtown Donalsonville. Registered voters can vote in advance at the probation judge's office in the courthouse from 8:30 am - 5 pm, Mon - Fri until Dec 31. Any voters needing special accommodations can call the election superintendent’s office at 229-524-5256. Read more.

Spotlight

Sales of living Christmas trees are up this year due to C-19. GA’s tree crop is in great shape for it, thanks to extra rain earlier in the year. Most trees will last up to a month if kept watered, so it isn’t too early to get one for Christmas.

Locally, Springhill Tree Farm is open daily through Dec 18, with both choose and cut local field trees and pre-cut trees from North Carolina. Springhill offers Carolina Sapphire, Frasier Fir, Virginia Pine, and Leyland Cypress trees. Read more.
