Friday, February 05, 2021
News

TRI-STATE AREA

  1. The Leon Co Health Department has confirmed at least 2 cases of the COVID-19 B.1.1.7 variant in Leon county. The strain was first identified in the United Kingdom and is significantly more contagious than the original strain. Epidemiologists are contact tracing and urge the public to continue to mask, wash hands, socially distance, and follow isolation and quarantine recommendations. Read more.

    Related: GA Public Health Commissioner Dr. Kathleen Toomey stated that the highly contagious variant is “probably widespread” throughout the state. As of Wednesday, 23 cases had been confirmed in GA. Read more.

STATEWIDE

  1. GA has administered over 1 million C-19 vaccine doses, 66% of the federal government's total shipment. Demand for the vaccine continues to outpace supply, but the state’s allocation is set to bump up to 154K doses per week with next week’s shipment. GA is still in Phase 1A of the vaccine rollout - first responders, healthcare workers, long-term care facility residents and staff, and individuals over 65 are eligible. Read more.

    Locally:

    • The Seminole Co Health Department has vaccinated nearly 1,000 residents with their first dose of the C-19 vaccine. Read more.
    • Grady Co Emergency Management plans to open a drive-thru vaccination site at Cairo High School when more vaccines are available. Read more.
    • Primary Care of SWGA is taking names for their C-19 vaccine waiting list; appointments will start as soon as supplies are available. Read more.

DECATUR CO.

  1. St. John’s Episcopal Church is holding a food drive Feb 8-14 to fill the church’s Little Free Pantry. Non-perishable, non-expired food items (including baby food and formula) can be dropped off at 516 E Broughton St in Bainbridge on Feb 12, and 15 from 9 am - 5 pm. Suggested items include canned fruits and vegetables, boxed milk, peanut butter, rice, pasta, and even spices. The pantry is open 24 hours a day, 7 days a week for those in need or those who want to contribute. Read more.
