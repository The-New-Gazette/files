Spotlight

Ruby-throated hummingbirds are back in our region to nest and raise their young, the only species that does so in North America. Both males and females are metallic green and white; the males have the characteristic ruby throat. You can draw them to your yard with pollinator gardens or artificial feeders filled with homemade sugar water - don’t use honey or red dye! St. Francis Wildlife gets calls every year from homeowners trying to help the tiny birds in trouble - birds knocked out after running into windows, caught in screens, or trapped in garages - you can reach them at 850-627-4151 for advice.
