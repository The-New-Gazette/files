FLORIDA

  1. Almost 750 West Indian manatees have died in FL this year. Agricultural runoff, microplastics, rising temperatures, severe weather, and deteriorating water treatment infrastructure have diminished food sources and contributed to the unusual mortality event (UME). Marine biologist Duane De Freese calls the UME a “symptom of a system under stress” that points to broader water quality and conservation issues in need of urgent solutions. Read more.

Related: The National Fish and Wildlife Foundation is funding the restoration of seven acres of seagrass in St. Andrews Bay as part of a $2M restoration project. Seagrass provides critical habitat and food sources for many marine species, including manatees. Read more.

SOUTHWEST GEORGIA

  1. For some rural SWGA communities, access to local healthcare is even more limited than before the coronavirus pandemic. Southwest GA Regional Medical Center in Cuthbert stopped accepting C-19 patients early in the pandemic and officially closed last October due to financial difficulties. As a result, Terrell and Randolph County residents must now travel across state lines to AL or up to an hour away to Albany for emergency care. Across the U.S., communities near closed hospitals suffered significantly higher death rates than the rest of their states. Read more.
