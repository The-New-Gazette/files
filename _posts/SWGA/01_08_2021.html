STATEWIDE

  1. The latest Whitehouse Coronavirus Task Force state report calls for GA to implement further measures to curb the spread of C-19. GA is currently ninth in the country for new cases and eighth in test positivity rates, but second to last in vaccine rollout. 97% of all GA counties have high levels of community transmission. The report recommends uniform implementation of face masking, increased testing, and speeding up vaccination efforts. Read more or view the report.

    More: State health experts call for tighter restrictions as hospitals across GA reach capacity and move to ICU/critical care diversion. Read more.

    Locally: Donalsonville doctors urge everyone to follow CDC guidelines and speak about local vaccine efforts and pandemic impacts. Read more.

  2. The 2020 election season ended on Tuesday in GA, with record early and mail-in voter turnout for Senate runoffs. Democrat Jon Ossoff defeated incumbent David Purdue and Reverend Raphael Warnock defeated incumbent Kelly Loeffler, giving GA two Democrat Senators in the U.S. Senate. Lauren “Bubba” McDonald defeated Daniel Blackman for GA Public Service Commissioner. Read more.

    Related: GA Secretary of State Brad Raffensperger addressed false claims about GA elections in a 10-page letter yesterday. Read more.

    Locally: View Decatur Co. election results here. Read about the election in Seminole Co. here.

SWGA

  1. Phase 1A for C-19 vaccinations has expanded in GA to adults aged 65 and older, law enforcement, and first responders. The SWGA Public Health District will offer the vaccines to the expanded group starting Jan 11, by appointment only. Call 229-352-6567 to schedule. Read more.

    More: You can find info about vaccine priority allocation here.

  2. Decatur Co. Sheriff’s Office is offering a $200 reward for information leading to the arrest of a suspect who fired shots in a residential area on Sunday. The suspect fired two 9mm rounds at 8 pm at the RV Center on Dothan Rd. Call Adam Mobley (229-400-8011) or Elijah McCoy (229-400-8004) with information. Read more.
