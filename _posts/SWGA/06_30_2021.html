SWGA

  1. A federal jury convicted a Camilla resident last week for Animal Welfare Act violations in the operation of a dogfighting ring. The defendant faces a maximum of five years in prison for 15 counts of aiding and abetting the possession and training of dogs for purposes of an animal fighting venture. Eleven co-defendants entered guilty pleas to related charges. Read more.

DECATUR COUNTY

  1. Bainbridge Public Safety responded to two fires at Elberta Crate last week. The first small fire broke out in one of the factory’s wood milling machines when a bearing went out. Embers from the first fire made it to the dust bin, which caught fire an hour later. Both fires were extinguished, and no one was injured. Read more.

Spotlight

Cuthbert-born master leather artist Winfred Rembert died on March 31st of this year at the age of 75. He picked cotton as a child, spent time active in the civil rights movement as a teen, and spent seven years on a chain gang in Morgan, GA. While on the chain gang, he spent some time digging ditches and fighting fire ants in Bainbridge. He began carving and painting his memories into leather at the age of 51. Before his death, he spent time telling his story to Erin I. Kelly of the New Yorker. Read more.
