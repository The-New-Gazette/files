STATEWIDE

  1. Gov. Kemp extended COVID-19 orders on Monday to renew the Public Health State of Emergency and allow nurses and pharmacists to administer the pending COVID-19 vaccine in drive-thru settings. He expects the vaccine to be available later this month for healthcare workers. Read more.

    Locally: The SWGA Health District has started preparations for local vaccine distribution, including ordering special refrigeration equipment and planning for drive-thru vaccine events. Read more.

    Phoebe Putney’s chief medical information officer explains more about the vaccine here.

  2. Monday, Dec 7, is the deadline to register to vote in the Jan 5 runoff election. Jan 1 is the last day to request an absentee ballot. In Decatur Co., advanced early voting will be at the Courthouse Annex Mon - Fri, Dec 14 - Dec 31, with Sat voting on Dec 19. The annex will be closed on Dec 24, Dec 25, and Jan 1. Register to vote here, check your voter status here or get your absentee ballot here.

BAINBRIDGE

  1. This year’s local Walk to End Alzheimer’s will continue raising funds through Dec 31. Fundraising did not occur in person this year due to C-19; instead, participants walked alone or in small groups on sidewalks and trails around Bainbridge. The GA chapter continues to offer education and support resources online. You can donate or register to walk here. Read more.

  2. A Bainbridge teen born without hands helped lead the Bearcats to victory at last weekend’s playoff game. The BHS varsity football team won the playoff game against Westside of Macon on Sat. Freshman Jamauri Williams’ five-yard catch and positive attitude went viral on social media. Read more.
