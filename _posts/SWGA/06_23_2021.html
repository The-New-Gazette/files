A good ending.

Good afternoon dear readers and neighbors! While 2020 was a perfect time to experiment with streamlining the delivery of relevant local news, we have determined that the service is not viable over the long term at this time. It was our sincere pleasure to provide this service for a season, and we hope you found it helpful in staying informed and connected. Our last edition will be next week.

If you were one of our generous donors, we are very grateful for your support. We will be canceling any recurring donations as of today. If you would like to send a note of thanks to our local writer who has done an amazing job, feel free to respond to this email, and we will forward it on.

We wish you all the very best and hope you continue to engage and live well in Southwest GA.

Kindest Regards,

Jared W - The New Gazette Editor

STATEWIDE

  1. The GA Secretary of State is set to remove almost 102K voter files from registration rolls in the latest statewide maintenance effort. People who respond to mailed cancellation notices within 40 days can have their registration returned to active; voters with canceled registrations can register again with updated information. DECATUR CO.

  1. Bainbridge City Council unanimously approved a change to downtown traffic patterns in their regularly scheduled meeting on Tuesday, June 15. Broughton Street will change to one-way westbound, Water Street will run one-way eastbound, and Clark Street will run one-way northbound. Safety concerns, a parking study, and the recommendations of City Engineer Gabe Menendez influenced the decision; multiple store owners in the affected area voiced opposition. Read more.

  2. The Decatur County Board of Education is purchasing a drug dog for the Decatur County School System. The dog will be trained in Dooley County until it is old enough for patrol, and Officer Leroy Akins will attend training. This purchase follows an earlier decision to send students to an alternative school if found on campus with a vape. Read more.
