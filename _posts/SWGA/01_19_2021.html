STATEWIDE

  1. Gov. Brian Kemp signed an order Friday providing new public health guidance and authorizing dentists to administer COVID-19 vaccines. The executive order also updated restaurant protocols and suspended a GA law that prohibited administering vaccines to people while in their cars. Read more.

    Extra: View the executive order.

  2. The GA Department of Public Health seeks volunteers to help with the state’s C-19 vaccination efforts. Even those with no medical background can help. The Georgia Responds volunteer program matches licensed medical and non-medical volunteers to skills-appropriate roles, including administering vaccines, language translation, data entry, and other assistance at vaccine sites. Read more.

SWGA

  1. January is on track to be the deadliest month for C-19 deaths in Dougherty Co., outpacing the number of fatalities in Spring. At a C-19 task meeting last Friday, Albany Mayor Bo Dorough announced that his father recently passed due to C-19 complications. County leaders and health professionals implore people to follow CDC guidelines, continue to mask and social distance. Watch more.

  2. GA Power Co. will start draining the Plant Mitchell coal ash ponds next month and release the treated water into the Flint River in Dougherty Co. Two million tons of coal ash from the decommissioned plant will be recycled into Portland Cement and other construction projects. Three ponds will be drained, filled in, and planted with grass. Third-parties will monitor water quality near the plant. Read more.
