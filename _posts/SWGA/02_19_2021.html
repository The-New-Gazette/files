STATEWIDE

  1. GA state officials are finalizing plans to expand the list of eligible COVID-19 vaccine recipients while four mass vaccination sites are set up across the state. Educators are hoping school employees will be on the expanded priority list as more schools return to in-person instruction - the decision will be made over the next two weeks. Mass vaccination events will be held in Albany, Macon, Habersham Co, and at the Atlanta airport. C-19 cases are on the decline, but GA is sixth in the nation for C-19 related deaths, and most counties do not currently meet CDC guidance for community transmission rates compatible with safe school re-opening. Read more.

    Related: Many states have prioritized educators in vaccination efforts, and some GA teachers have crossed state lines to take advantage of this expanded eligibility in AL. Read more.

    Locally:

    • The Bainbridge Walmart is now accepting appointments to vaccinate those in group 1A. It offers C-19 vaccines to the general public after scheduled appointments have been met as part of a “no vaccines wasted” policy. Schedule now.

DECATUR CO

  1. A child was life-flighted to Tallahassee Memorial Hospital, and an arrest has been made after a DUI crash in Bainbridge on Wednesday evening. A 5-year-old boy was hit by a motor vehicle while riding his bicycle on Broughton St and received substantial injuries. Investigation of the incident was turned over to the GA State Patrol Specialized Collision Reconstruction Team. A woman has been arrested and charged with serious injury by vehicle, DUI, and driving with a suspended or revoked license. Read more.
