STATEWIDE

  1. GA renters who are behind on rent payments due to the COVID-19 pandemic may now apply for assistance through a new application portal from the GA Department of Community Affairs. The GA Rental Assistance Program provides up to 15 months of utility and rental assistance for those who qualify. Funds will be distributed directly to landlords to bring past due rent and utility payments current, and both landlords and tenants can apply. Visit the portal to apply or learn more.

  2. This week is Flood Safety Preparedness Week in GA. There have been 644 flooding events in the state since January of 2015, causing over $20 million in damages, and flooding kills more people than all other weather hazards. The 2009 floods remain one of the most costly events in modern GA history. Read more.

SWGA

  1. Vaccine efforts in southwest GA outpace the rest of the state. While Albany is near the top of CDC vaccination rankings, the state as a whole is last in the nation. One year ago the city was a global hotspot of C-19 cases. The majority-Black community saw a disproportionate number of cases and deaths, and the area still faces epidemics of C-19 comorbidities like diabetes and hypertension. Transportation issues and age restrictions remain obstacles for many who are the most at-risk. Read more.

Spotlight

The Albany Museum of Art currently hosts three new exhibitions featuring Southern artists:

  • Butch Anthony: Art, Nature and Intertwangleism is running in the Haley Gallery from Mar 4 - Jul 31. Anthony is a multimedia artist from AL and FL who utilizes found objects and diverse techniques to explore identity themes and illustrate collected stories.
  • Works, Worship and Community features 29 photographs from the Decatur Co master-photographer Paul Kwilecki and can be found in the East Gallery now through Jun 26.
  • Educators as Artists features artworks by university and college educators in South GA and North FL. The exhibit includes works from 14 teaching artists at schools within 100 miles of Albany and can be seen in the McCormack Gallery until Jun 26.
