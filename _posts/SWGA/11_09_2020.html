IMPORTANT

  1. Tropical Storm Eta made landfall in the lower FL keys last night with heavy rainfall and gusty winds. The Big Bend and South GA are in the cone of possible impacts from rain, wind, and storm surge later this week. Read more or view the latest NHS advisory.

  2. GA has two runoff elections dates coming up. Dec. 1 is the runoff for state and local races. The runoff election for two GA U.S. Senate seats is Jan. 5. Read more.

    More: The registration deadline for Dec. elections has passed. Nov. 18th is the deadline to request an absentee ballot for the Jan. race. The voter registration deadline is Dec. 7th. Early in-person voting begins Dec. 14th. Read more or request your absentee ballot here.

SWGA

  1. Anthem Blue Cross Blue Shield (BCBS) of GA will be terminating its contracts with multiple hospital systems in SWGA next year. On Friday, Colquitt Regional Medical Center announced that the insurance provider is terminating its contract with the hospital on Jan. 1, 2021, and its contracts with all Archbold Health System hospitals. Read more.

  2. The cities of Colquitt, Donalsonville, Leesburg, and Moultrie were recently designated Rural Zones as part of a collaboration between the GA Department of Community Affairs and the GA Department of Economic Development. The program includes job tax credits, investment credits, and rehabilitation credits to encourage investments. Read more.

MILLER CO.

  1. Miller Co. voters reelected deceased incumbent Scott Worsley for sheriff last week. Worsley died of cancer on Oct. 27. Chief Deputy Rober Frier will serve as interim sheriff until the county attorney makes a final decision. Read more.
