STATEWIDE

  1. The GA General Assembly may ask GA voters to weigh in on daylight savings time. Sen. Ben Watson and Rep. Wes Cantrell have called for a non-binding advisory question that will ask voters if the state should keep observing the annual time changes, switch to year-round standard time, or switch to year-round daylight savings time. The state Senate passed legislation back in March to call for a non-binding referendum on the issue, but the measure died when the General Assembly shut down for C-19. Read more.

  2. GA Chief Justice Harold D. Melton suspended jury trials in GA due to the C-19 spread. Any in-person trials currently in progress may be completed in person at the local judge’s discretion. The order urges all other proceedings to happen remotely whenever possible. Read more.

  1. C-19 vaccines are now available from the SWGA Public Health District. The department is currently vaccinating Phase 1A, including healthcare workers and residents of long term care facilities. Healthcare organizations and congregate living settings can request vaccines from Public Health. Read more.
