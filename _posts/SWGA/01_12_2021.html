STATEWIDE

  1. GA lawmakers returned to work at the state Capitol Monday under guard and new C-19 mandates. A state police SWAT team was posted outside due to last week’s unrest at the federal capital and threats of violent protests at state capitols across the nation. Masks and twice-weekly saliva tests are now required in both chambers in an attempt to curb the spread of C-19. Read more.

    More: Read a detailed thread of GA lawmakers’ first day in session here.

  2. GA County health departments have been overwhelmed with requests for the C-19 vaccine since the Phase 1A category was expanded to firefighters, law enforcement, first responders, and people over 65. GA is currently last amongst the states in COVID vaccine distribution. Read more.

    More: The GA Department of Public Health has launched a website to allow users to search for private and public vaccine providers. View here.

    SWGA: Phoebe Putney Health System began vaccinating the expanded Phase 1A group on Monday at hospitals in Albany, Americus, and Sylvester. Read more. Memorial Manor in Bainbridge has temporarily suspended vaccination appointments due to supply limitations. Read more. SWGA Public Health District is accepting appointments for C-19 testing and vaccination by phone at 229-352-6567.

DECATUR COUNTY

  1. Memorial Hospital and Manor in Bainbridge are accepting blood donations tomorrow, Wednesday, Jan 13, 11 am - 4 pm. Donors will receive a $10 gift card, wellness checkup, and C-19 antibody test. Appointments are encouraged; call 1-888-9-DONATE.

  2. Bainbridge Municipal Court cases scheduled for Jan 20 are canceled due to high C-19 risk. Cases will be rescheduled, and citations can be paid at City Hall during regular business hours. This notice does not include Decatur Co. Superior, State, or Juvenile court. Individuals with upcoming court dates in those courts should consult their attorneys or call 229-248-3025 for updates. Find more info at the Decatur Co. Clerk’s Office.
