News

STATEWIDE

  1. The GA State Election Board has extended two emergency rules for absentee voting in the next election. The use of the monitored and secured drop off boxes is extended until the Jan. 5 runoff. Counties are now required to process absentee ballots starting one week before Election Day. Read more.

    Related: Residents have until Dec. 7 to register to vote in the GA Senate runoff elections. Read more.

  2. The GA Access to Medical Cannabis Commission has started the process to find growers and manufacturers for medical cannabis oil. A limited number of licenses will be granted for the plant's indoor growth exclusively for conversion into low-THC oil. Possession of low-THC oil by registered patients was legalized in 2015, but patients had no legal path to obtain it without in-state production. Read more.

TRI-STATE AREA

  1. The Jackson Co. Sheriff’s Office is still investigating last week’s fatal dog attack in rural Bascom. Investigation and an autopsy confirmed the victim, Donald Allen, was attacked by dogs and died from his injuries. Investigators have released photos of the pack of dogs they believe may be responsible and continue to look for dogs traveling in packs in the area. Read more.

SWGA

  1. The electronic recount of votes from the Nov. 3 presidential election is officially underway in south GA. The recount was requested by the Trump campaign and will be paid for by GA taxpayers. A hand recount has already been completed, and the state’s results were certified Friday, showing a win for Joe Biden. Counties have until midnight on Dec. 2 to complete the recount. Read more.

    More: The Decatur Co. Board of Elections will begin their recount at 9 am on Mon., Nov. 30 at 122 W. Water St. in Bainbridge. Members of the public may be present. Read more.
