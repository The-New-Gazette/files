MADISON COUNTY

  1. Emergency rental assistance is still on hold for Madison County residents. The County Commission passed an Emergency Rental Assistance Resolution on Wednesday to outline how the county will distribute the $5.2M in federal funding received in January. The county is not yet open for applications and is in the process of hiring a program administrator. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. HSV City Council has scheduled two meetings to discuss the Police Citizens Advisory Council findings from last summer’s downtown protests. A lawyer representing the advisory Council will present the findings at a meeting on April 22 that is open for public comment. A second meeting on April 28 will serve as a work session for city council members and be closed for public comment. Read more.

  2. A new scholarship opportunity opened this week for aspiring nurses. The Daisy P. Swinton scholarship is named in honor of HSV Hospital’s first African-American ICU Nurse Manager. Swinton started her 40-year hospital career in the housekeeping department before pursuing further education to become an LPN and eventually a Registered Nurse. Read more.

  3. The Beach Company broke ground on Wednesday on two new West HSV developments. The Range is a modern Class-A office building; The Foundry is a mixed-use development with retail, restaurant, and living space. The new developments will work closely with the Stovehouse and are projected to open in Summer 2022. Read more.
