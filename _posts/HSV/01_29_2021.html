HUNTSVILLE

  1. The City of HSV defended vaccinating some community leaders at a public event after facing a backlash. The mayor, pastors from four predominantly Black churches, and the president of Oakwood University received the vaccine as part of an effort to demonstrate the vaccine’s safety to those who are hesitant. Some community members accused the group of “cutting in line” and taking limited vaccine supplies before they were eligible. Read more.

    More: Mayor Battle addressed constituent concerns about limited supplies and explained the event was in response to canvassing that indicated 60% of the Black community surveyed did not trust the vaccine. Read more.

  2. HSV City Council approved a $6M federal grant last night to assist HSV citizens with rent and utility payments who have lost income due to the pandemic. The Federal Emergency Rental Assistance grant funds provide rental assistance to eligible families who reside in HSV. Madison Co. received a separate, smaller grant allocation for its residents. Read more.

REDSTONE ARSENAL

  1. The US Air Force paid out $787.2M to Northrop Grumman and Blue Origin to officially end U.S. Space Force partnerships with the companies. The launch service agreements ended on Dec 31, after the two companies lost the Phase 2 contracts to SpaceX and ULA. Blue Origin’s BE-4 engine will power the Vulcan Centaur launch vehicle from ULA in Phase 2 missions. Read more.

    Context: ULA’s manufacturing, assembly, and integration operations are located in Decatur, and Blue Origin recently opened a new engine assembly plant here in HSV.

  2. A federal lawsuit has been filed against the HSV city school board and five school system employees for extreme bullying of a special needs student. The suit alleges three students bullied and physically attacked an 8-year-old boy at Mountain Gap Elementary School in late 2016 and early 2017. Attorneys for the child’s mother asked for a jury trial and compensatory and punitive damages for the injuries the child received. Read more.

Spotlight

A new, inclusive coffee shop is opening at Stovehouse in West HSV this weekend. Charlie Foster’s serves coffee, drinks, and food while providing employment opportunities for the differently-abled.

Co-owners Austin and Holly Jenkins started the business after years of working with the special needs community through Merrimack Hall Performing Arts Center. Half of the staff have intellectual or developmental disabilities.

The shop has been in soft opening since mid-January to prepare employees. The soft opening continues today from 7 am - 3 pm, and the grand opening is Saturday, Jan 30. Read more.
