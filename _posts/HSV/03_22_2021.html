NORTH ALABAMA

  1. An armed home burglary in HSV led to a car-chase spanning five counties and finally ended in Maury County, TN. The suspect was eventually cornered in a stolen vehicle and, after pulling a gun, was shot by the police, taken to a hospital for treatment, and remains in police custody. No other parties were injured. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. HSV Police’s Internal Affairs Dept. moved to a separate building on N. Memorial Pkwy for greater public accessibility. The department director intends that the new location will reduce anxiety and provide both the public and HPD officers with more privacy when reporting complaints of misconduct against HPD personnel. Read more.

  2. Pre-registration is now open for HSV City Schools Virtual Academy. Pre-registration is not a commitment to the virtual program, but it is mandatory for those who wish to enroll in the 2021–2022 school year program. Read more.

  3. MidCity District developers broke ground on the first residential building, called the Metronome. It will be a five-floor building with 296 apartments, featuring retail and restaurant spaces, including a Trader Joe’s on the ground floor. Leasing begins in 2022. Read more.
