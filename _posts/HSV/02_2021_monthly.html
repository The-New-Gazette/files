IMPORTANT

  1. C-19 cases have been dropping across Madison County over the past few weeks as more people receive the C-19 vaccine. While cases are dropping, there have also been issues with vaccine dose availability. Read more.

    Context: See the latest data here and check the AL Department of Public Health (ADPH) online portal for vaccine eligibility and appointments here.

  2. After 42 years in office, AL Sen. Richard Shelby announced he would not seek re-election in 2022. Shelby is the longest-serving Alabamian in the U.S. In Shelby’s retirement, Alabama loses a senior ranking Senator who has fueled a large amount of defense spending to the state. Read more.

NORTH ALABAMA

  1. Six Alabama educators from the Athens City Schools and Limestone County Schools have been charged in a $7M Federal fraud and conspiracy scheme. The FBI and state officials stated that the defendants fraudulently enrolled full-time private students in the system’s virtual schools to get more state funding. Read more.

    More: You can read the full 80-page indictment here.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. The Huntsville Venue Group (HVG) announced Tuesday that the Huntsville Amphitheater at MidCity would open in April 2022. HVG plans for the 8,000 capacity venue to be utilized year-round for music, community building opportunities, and gatherings. Read more.

    More: Mumford & Sons’ Ben Lovett, who founded the Venue Group, has been personally involved in the process of bringing the new amphitheater to HSV. Read more. 

     

  2. AL AG Steve Marshall announced that the state is receiving $9.2M in a landmark multi-state settlement with McKinsey & Company for its harmful promotion of opioids. Alabama has the highest opioid prescription rate in the nation and resulted in many opioid-related deaths. The City of Huntsville is seeking a portion of those funds and filed its own lawsuit against McKinsey & Company in 2019. Read more.

    Context: In 2019, three separate Madison County doctors were charged for knowingly prescribing opioids to drug addicts, for recruiting sex workers to become patients, and signing blank prescription forms for staffers to fill out. Read more.

  3. Work has begun on the Constellation mixed-used development off Clinton Ave in downtown HSV. The project is touted as a new “front door” for downtown. Plans include an apartment complex, parking garages, retail, and an office building which could range between 13 to 20 floors, potentially making it the tallest building downtown. Read more. 

CITY OF MADISON

  1. Madison City Council unanimously voted to form a Police Citizens Advisory Committee to promote dialogue between the community and law enforcement. The nine-member committee will serve in an advisory capacity to the city police department on officer training, quality of life issues, and community concerns. Council members, Mayor Paul Finley, and the Chief of Police will appoint committee members and are currently accepting applications for the positions. Read more.

    More: Committee members must be at least 18 years old, reside in Madison, and be willing to serve for two to four years. Apply here.

REDSTONE ARSENAL

  1. The Dept. of Defense Inspector General announced that it would review the decision to relocate the Space Command HQ. The evaluation will be based on whether the decision to move the command to HSV complied with the Dept. of Defense and Air Force policies, used objective and relevant scoring factors, and calculated the cost accurately and consistently among all candidates. Read more.
