HUNTSVILLE

  1. HSV City Schools have returned to in-person classes five days a week. The district began the year remotely on August 17th and remained virtual through mid-September. The school district says they will remain mindful of the ongoing public health crisis and maintain a flexible approach should conditions worsen. Read more.

  2. Encompass Health breaks ground on a new inpatient rehabilitation hospital in North HSV. The hospital has been serving the community from its current location on Governors Drive since 1987, seeing more than 45,000 patients over the last 30 years. The new location, slated for completion in the spring of 2022, will include 76 rooms for patients recovering from complex orthopedic conditions. Read more.

Spotlight

The site of Councill High School, the first high school for African-American students in HSV, has reopened as the city’s newest park. Originally founded in the basement of the Lakeside Methodist Episcopal Church in 1867, the school transitioned to its own building on the site in 1892, where it remained until desegregation led to its closure in 1966. Every aspect of the new park represents something from the old school; bricks from the school building are dotted around the site, and picnic tables are sited where the lunchroom used to be. Read more.
