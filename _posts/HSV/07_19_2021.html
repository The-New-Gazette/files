  1. In Sheriff Mike Blakely’s corruption trial, counts one through four have been dismissed. These counts concerned thefts totaling $11,000 from his campaign account. Blakely still faces theft and ethics felonies. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. A junior at UAH filed a lawsuit against the school, arguing that their policies don’t align with the state’s Campus Free Speech Act. The student, President of Young Americans for Liberty at UAH, says these policies require the group to get administrative approval before speaking on campus about current events. Read more.

  2. The City of HSV has created a new, full-time position called the HSV Music Officer. The new unique role is one of the outcomes of Mayor Tommy Battle’s Music Initiative, an effort to make HSV a name in the concert scene and boost the local economy. Learn more and apply for the position here.

  3. In honor of the first anniversary of the death of Congressman John Lewis, a vigil was held Saturday morning. Some of the organizers are working to gain support for the John Lewis Voting Rights Advancement Act. If passed, it would restore a removed part of the 1965 Voting Rights Act that required jurisdictions with a history of racial discrimination to acquire approval before changing their voting rules. Read more.

Spotlight

The World’s Longest Yard Sale is returning this year, August 5 through 8, and will span almost 700 miles, from Gadsden, AL, to Addison, Michigan. Alabama is expecting more than 1,000 local vendors, and our section will be along Lookout Mountain Parkway. You’ll be able to find antiques, collectibles, furniture, dishware, local produce, and even live entertainment. Of course, there’s a lot of other fun to be had at Lookout Mountain as well, from waterfalls to canyons to national parks. Read more.
