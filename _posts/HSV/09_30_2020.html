MADISON COUNTY

  1. The Toyota USA Foundation has awarded $200K for e-learning in North Alabama and launched a hub for virtual education. The grant will be spread across all three school districts in the metro area for devices and internet access to support students with distance learning. The local investment is part of a larger initiative supporting education in the 13 states where Toyota operates. Read more.

    More: The virtual education hub provides virtual field trips and STEM-based lessons for young people interested in engineering. Check it out here.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. Remington Outdoors was broken up in a bankruptcy auction this week. Seven companies won the bidding for various pieces of the 200-year old business, including the Remington brand. The proceeds of the auction will bring in $155M, which will be applied against the company’s significant debts, including $12.5M owed collectively to the City of HSV, Madison County, and the HSV Industrial Development Board. Read more.

    More: Here is HSV Mayor Tommy Battle’s reaction to the bankruptcy auction.

  2. Lincoln Mill is undergoing an extensive restoration and redevelopment project slated for completion late next year. The redevelopment aims to enhance the beauty and usability of the historic property and will include new dining and retail spaces. The mill is just minutes from downtown HSV and promises to create further connections between the city center and north HSV. Read more.

  3. An anti-mask lawsuit filed by a former judge, Roy Moore, moved from HSV to Montgomery. The suit challenges Gov. Ivey’s mandatory mask ordinance, arguing that it restricts freedoms guaranteed under the Constitution. However, on Monday, federal judge, Liles Burke, held that the case should be tried where the actions of the defendants were taken. As Gov. Ivey lives and works in Montgomery, the case will move to the U.S District Court for the Middle District. Read more.

Spotlight

Famed American photographer, Jonathan Becker, has spent more than four decades capturing the vibrancy and solemnity of the world around us. His photography has been a staple of Vanity Fair’s glossy pages since 1981, and he has contributed to the New Yorker, Vogue, Town & Country, among other esteemed publications. Spanning the globe and transecting society, Becker’s images provide an enduring visual history of change.

A new exhibition entitled “Jonathan Becker: Social Work - Four Decades of Movers, Shakers, and Thinkers” opens at the HSV Museum of Art on October 30. Learn more.
