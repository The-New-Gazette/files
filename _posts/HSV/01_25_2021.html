NORTH ALABAMA

  1. The North Alabama Drug Task Force seized over $400K worth of illegal drugs and weapons off the streets this week. The confiscated materials include marijuana, methamphetamine, cocaine, heroin, MDMA, fentanyl, steroids, handguns, a rifle-style pistol, and more than $18k in cash. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. The Air Force has defended HSV as their preliminary choice for U.S. Space Command. State and city officials from other competing locations for the U.S. Space command have claimed that President Trump intervened and forced the decision to reward the new HQ to a city represented by his political supporters Rep. Brooks and Senator Tuberville. The Air Force Secretary has rebuffed this assertion that politics forced her decision and said that she thoughtfully considered input and feedback from various groups, and many different factors, before ultimately choosing HSV. Read more.

  2. Pints and Pixels, our local arcade bar, has moved to Campus 805 and re-opens this week. The new venue will introduce skee ball and old-school sodas into the mix, along with 33 beer taps and beer floats. While the venue will no longer provide food, they plan to introduce a service to pick up food from a list of nearby spots soon. Additionally, they’re sanitizing daily, limiting capacity to 25 people, and switching out tokens for a flat $10 fee for unlimited play. Read more.

  3. Two people were injured in a shooting Saturday morning outside the Sidetracks Music Hall at Twickenham Station. Police were called to the scene, and the two male victims, who have not been identified yet, were taken to the hospital. The investigation is ongoing. Read more.

  4. A car chase that started in Lincoln County, TN, ended in HSV when the pursued car hit a guardrail off South Memorial Pkwy around midnight on Saturday. No one was injured, and the three people in the car were arrested. What instigated the chase has not been provided yet. Read more.

QUICK BITS

  • 1600 people received the C-19 vaccine from HSV Hospital this past week, with distribution expanding over the next three weeks to 1,000 people a day. If you’re 75 or older, you can join the waitlist here.
  • MDS County will receive $5.2 million in federal aid for emergency rental and utility assistance and plans to distribute the aid to residents through local nonprofits and the county government. Read more.
  • In honor of Black History Month, the HSV Museum of Art will be showing an exhibit called Encounters by sculptor and printmaker Althea Murphy-Price until May 23. The exhibit explores the relationships among hair, social culture, and personal identity. Read more.
  • Hampton Cove Elementary, Montview Elementary, and Morris Elementary will be recognized by the AL State of Education for high performance and receive an award of $5,000. Read more.
  • Sparkman High Schools opened their new Fine Arts Center and have kicked it off with a production of Peter and the Starcatcher running from Jan. 21 - Jan 31. Read more.
