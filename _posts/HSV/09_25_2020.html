IMPORTANT

  1. State health officials announced that AL has not seen a significant spike in C-19 cases from Labor Day, suggesting that the statewide mask mandate has helped slow viral spread. There have been just over 11,000 new cases and 240 deaths in the state since the holiday. The rolling average of new daily cases has decreased by 8.6% in the last two weeks. Read more and view current state and local case counts here.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. HSV City Council voted unanimously last night to pass the proposed $236M budget for 2021. The city’s fiscal year runs Oct. 1 - Sept. 30. The budget includes funds for a new fire station, road maintenance, two new city buses, the initial phase of the new city hall, and a 1% living pay increase for employees. Read more.

MADISON

  1. Madison City Schools released an updated school opening strategy yesterday. Middle school students begin a five-day schedule starting Sept. 28. James Clemens students start an A/B rotation on the 28th. Bob Jones students will not return until Oct. 12. Read more.

  2. Madison City Council is asking for public input on the future of the former youth home at the Three Springs Facility on Browns Ferry Road. The facility was purchased by the city earlier this year with plans to rebuild it as a community center. Madison residents can help decide future activity offerings via this survey. Read more.

REDSTONE ARSENAL

  1. The new Radio Frequency Identification lanes become active on Redstone Arsenal for some motorists beginning Oct. 1. Eligible drivers will be issued RFID cards and enter through Gate 0. Gates 1 and 7 will start using the new technology on Oct. 7. Read more.
