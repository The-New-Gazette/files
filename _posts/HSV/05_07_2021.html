HUNTSVILLE

  1. Update: HSV City Council members voiced support for reforms in response to the April 29 presentation of the Huntsville Police Citizens Advisory Council report. John Meredith suggested the city consider ending qualified immunity for officers. Devyn Keith argued for the release of body camera footage and recommended the police department hire a liaison. Bill Kling read a list of suggestions from the Citizens Coalition for Justice Reform. Read more.

    More: Review a summary of the 248-page HPCAC report here.

  2. UAH announced Wednesday that it is suspending the Charger hockey program after the team failed to secure a conference affiliation. The Central Collegiate Hockey Association did not accept UAH’s proposal, and the Atlantic Hockey Association has not yet responded. UAH has stated it will reinstate the program if the Chargers secure a conference membership, but the team will not be eligible to play for at least a year after the invitation. Read more.

CITY OF MADISON

  1. Interviews with the final four City of Madison Police Chief candidates are open to the public Saturday, May 8. The process begins at 8 am in City Council Chambers. Attendees may only enter at the beginning or leave at the end of each interview. Interviews will be recorded and available for public review no later than the afternoon of Monday, May 10. Read more.

  2. Rezoning updates: The Madison City Board of Education voted last night to approve the school rezoning plan proposed to accommodate the district’s new Midtown Elementary School. According to MCS Superintendent Dr. Ed Nichols, the United States District Court that presides over a 60-year desegregation case has reviewed the plan and proposed no objections; the Board vote was the final step before starting student registrations. Additional rezoning is planned to accommodate the opening of Journey Middle School in the Fall of 2022 and will follow a similar process to the elementary school rezoning. Read more.

Spotlight

The new Community Foundation Park at the intersection of Dr. Joseph Lowery Boulevard and Pelham Avenue is now open in downtown HSV. The park is a collaboration between the city, Arts HSV, and the Community Foundation of Greater HSV. The park plaza features a sitting area and a public art installation from RDG Dahlquist Art Studio, the first of three to be installed this year. Read more.
