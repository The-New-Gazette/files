IMPORTANT

  1. Gov. Ivey announced that Alabama will end all federal pandemic unemployment compensation programs effective June 19, 2021. According to Ivey, unemployment assistance was only meant to be a “short-term relief program” and is now “contributing to a labor shortage that is compromising the continuation of our economic recovery.” Read more.

    Context: Federal unemployment is currently $350 to $575 a week, while state unemployment is $44 to $275 a week. Around 69,000 people who received unemployment benefits last week would no longer qualify once Alabama ends its participation in federal programs.

  1. The Colonial Pipeline, which halted operations after last week's ransomware attack, is keeping its mainlines offline and running smaller lateral lines. When asked about possible gas shortages, Clay Ingram with AAA Alabama said he “doesn’t expect the cyber attack to impact our supply all that much.” Read more.

MADISON COUNTY

  1. William Darby Conviction Updates:
    • HSV Police Officer William Darby was placed on paid administrative leave after his murder conviction last week. Read more.
    • Darby’s attorney announced plans to appeal the jury’s decision while Jefferey Parker’s family will pursue a civil lawsuit against Darby and the City of HSV. Read more.
    • AL Democratic Party Executive Director Wade Perry called for the firing of both HSV Police Chief Mark McMurray and Darby after Darby was placed on paid administrative leave. Read more.
  2. The Madison County school system is experiencing higher numbers of teacher retirements and resignations than usual. Director of Personnel Kenneth Kubik ties this to the extra requirements of teachers in the shift to online learning during the pandemic. There will also be 70 new positions in the upcoming school year due to secured Emergency Education Relief funds. The 141 current job openings are for teachers, drivers, administration, and other school support staff. Read more.

MADISON

  1. Trash Pandas fans saw traffic delays, some of up to two hours, when entering Toyota Field for last night’s sold-out inaugural home game. The one-lane entrance to the parking lot clogged, causing some fans to park on the road and walk to the game on foot. Lindsey Knupp, Trash Pandas VP of Marketing, said staff monitored the situation and will work with the city to alleviate the traffic issues. The Pandas lost 4-3 to the Tennessee Smokies. Read more.
