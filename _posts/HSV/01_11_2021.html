STATEWIDE

  1. C-19 vaccines will be available by appointment to anyone 75 years old and older as well as first responders starting Jan. 18. Appointments can be made by calling the ADPH hotline at (855) 566-5333 instead of calling local hospitals directly. Read more.

    More: HSV Hospital has a form to join the vaccine waiting list here. Meanwhile, Crestwood is not currently taking vaccination appointments due to the available supply.

  2. Governor Kay Ivey supports a peaceful transition of power in national elections. Gov. Ivey has said peaceful transitions are a “hallmark” of democracy, which must be upheld. Meanwhile, she has opposed efforts to oust President Trump by impeachment or the 25th Amendment because it would incite further division and violence. Read more.

    More: When asked to comment on Rep. Mo Brooks, who refuses to apologize for his part in last Wednesday’s events, Gov. Ivey said 5th district voters could express their concerns to him directly and ultimately hold him accountable at the ballot box. She added that “he does not speak for all Republicans, much less all Alabamians.”

HUNTSVILLE

  1. The non-profit health clinic Clinica Medica Moscati opened on Lowe Ave. The founders hope the clinic, named after the patron saint of caretaking, will help many uninsured patients across HSV, especially those in the Hispanic community. Everyone on the staff is bilingual in English and Spanish. Learn more.

  2. North AL Land Trust has pulled plans for a nature center on Monte Sano. A group of residents passionately opposed the addition of a new nature center on the property near Tollgate Rd. New locations are now being considered for the center, which the organization says is urgently needed to inform people about the value of conserving nature and wild spaces. Read more.

Spotlight

A new business has opened on the downtown square called BeeZr (a play on the British slang term “beezer,” meaning excellent). BeeZr is a three-in-one establishment including Chandler’s Ford Brewing, Champagne Taco Kitchen, and Northside Coffee Roasters, and it will serve food from the vegan food truck Hippea Camper.

The kitchen will feature three standard tacos: a carnitas taco, a grilled halibut taco, and a white lump-crab taco with lime-saffron sauce. Other items will rotate through the menu periodically, including everything from pizza to gumbo to steak.

The brewery is designed to provide “bewildering variety” and can hold up to 25 different recipes fermenting at once. Its doors are open every day from 7 a.m. to 11 p.m., and its kitchen is open from 11 a.m. to 10 p.m.
