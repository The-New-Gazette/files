News

IMPORTANT

  1. Gov. Kay Ivey has extended the statewide mask order through August 31. The increase to almost 2,000 in-patient COVID-19 cases in Alabama convinced State Health Officer Dr. Scott Harris to advise the mask order extension. Read more.

  2. HSV Mayor Tommy Battle announced a $10m - $15m city budget deficit for 2020 as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic. City Finance Director Penny Smith reported that the city is in a stronger place than most and positive numbers for housing sales and permits. As a result of the deficit, city department heads have been asked to issue limited hiring freezes. Read more.

    Context: Read details of Smith’s report, as well as view her full presentation here.

NORTH ALABAMA

  1. The State of Alabama and the federal government have ended their months-long COVID-19 emergency moratorium on renter evictions. Local attorney Mark McDaniel advises renters to respond to eviction notices and exercise their right to a court hearing, which can gain time to pay rent and settle cases. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. In response to the possible permanent closure of the U.S. Space and Rocket Center, Boeing has donated $500K to the “Save Space Camp” campaign. The campaign has raised $1.24M of the $1.5M goal to keep the doors of HSV’s most popular tourist attraction open. Read more.

    More: The HSV-Madison County Chamber of Commerce is working to find additional resources for the facility, and Alabama Congressman Mo Brooks has co-sponsored a bill for loan programs for museums like the USSRC. Read more.

  2. The HSV Police Department has issued an official response to questions and suggestions submitted regarding local law enforcement actions at two downtown protests in June. While the department has tried to be transparent, some are concerned that actual policy change is limited. Meanwhile, the Huntsville Police Citizens Advisory Council (HPCAC), which is mentioned numerous times in the report, is seeking more independent authority as their role expands. Read more.

    More: Read the HPD’s response in full here.

    More: The HPCAC is currently reviewing statements submitted by citizens via emails and a Web form. Many are frustrated that the portal offers no option to upload photos and video footage of protest events. Read more.

QUICK BITS

  • Protests of the Confederate monument in downtown HSV continue. A group of representatives from nine historically Black sororities and fraternities have joined the call for the statue’s removal. Read more.
  • Those who have received suspicious seed packets from China via mail can now report it online to the Alabama Dept. of Agriculture. Submit a report.
  • With many state school districts prepare to open virtually this month, Gov. Kay Ivey has announced $100M in funds to aid low-income students who need high-speed internet access through the ABC program. Read more.
  • COVID-19 cases are on the rise at Tutt Fann Veteran’s Home; the staff has made several anonymous complaints about clean PPE availability. Read the veteran’s home response. Read more.
  • The Tennessee Valley Progressive Alliance organized a downtown HSV vigil to honor the passing of U.S. Congressman and civil rights icon John Lewis. Attendees saw projections of Lewis on HSV City Hall and reflected on his legacy. Read more.
Spotlight

Huntsville’s own Southern Fried Film Festival, a local celebration of filmmakers and creators throughout the Southeast, will be taking the festival to the MidCity drive-in theater this weekend! The festival will showcase seven films, including one main feature each night. There will also be live music, raffles, and refreshments while maintaining Safer at Home State guidelines.

Gates open at 6:00 pm with the films starting at 8:30 pm both nights. For more information, check out the festival on Twitter @SFFFest or southernfriedfilmfestival.org.
