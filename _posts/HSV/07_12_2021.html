News

HUNTSVILLE

  1. HSV City Council approved the second of two steel greenway bridges being installed in Brahan Spring Park. The bridges make connections between Brahan Spring Park, John Hunt Park, and the HSV Aquatics Center. Ultimately when everything is connected, there will be a greenway walking path from the cities largest parks, past Lowe Mill, and on to downtown. Read more.

  2. The Community Foundation has awarded its 2021 Racial Equity Fund grants to 11 recipients. The grants, which totaled over $70K, were distributed to organizations across town that are working to reduce gaps in racial equity and improve lives across the community. The three largest grants went to Village of Promise, Fantasy Playhouse Children’s Theatre & Academy, and Girls Inc. of Huntsville. Read more.

  3. The HSV PD is being criticized for asking employees to donate their leave time to an unspecified employee, supposedly William Darby, so that he can remain on the payroll. Darby was convicted two months ago for the murder of Jeffrey Parker. His sentencing is scheduled for August. Read more.

    More: The city cannot take Darby off payroll until they hold a disciplinary hearing. Read more.

  4. Despite 90 signatures on a petition, a developer bought a lot with one house to build two in its place. There’s no law against the development strategy according to the HSV Planning Commission, but it does violate the subdivision covenants and shows the influence of relentless development in the city. Read more.

Spotlight

Nature Play Camp, hosted by the Land Trust of North Alabama on the Monte Sano Nature Preserve, is coming next week as a last-minute, late-summer event for your kids. The camp will explore water critters, fossils, nature detectives, map reading, animal adoption, tracks, trees, and more. There will be crafts to create and games to play, and Fagan Creek to cool off in. The camp is for kids ages 7 to 11 and will occur from July 19 through 23 from 9 am to 4:30 pm. Registration just opened, so snag your kids’ spots before camp starts next week! Read more.
