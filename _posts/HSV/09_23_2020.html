IMPORTANT

  1. Dr. Neil Lamb, VP of Educational Outreach at HudsonAlpha, was named Chairman of the newly-established Alabama STEM Council by Gov. Kay Ivey. The Council aims to advise state leadership on developing an adept workforce to meet future labor demands in science, technology, and engineering. The focus on STEM is pertinent as the state continues to attract high-profile firms. The Council will be holding its first meeting within the next few months. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. HSV PD announced the opening of its state-of-the-art firearms training facility this week. Located on 40 acres off Wall Triana Hwy, the 14,000 sq ft facility is a joint partnership between the HSV PD and the FBI. With multiple ranges and a 7,500 sq ft tactical building, the multi-million dollar facility will provide high-level officer training. Read more.

  2. A new monument to Alabama’s Gold Star Families has been installed at the Huntsville-Madison County Veterans’ Memorial Park. Escorted to its final location by the Patriot Guard Riders, this monument will be the final installation at the Park. It is designed to honor both the Gold Star Families themselves, along with the legacy of their loved ones who died in service. Read more.

  3. HSV PD made an arrest in connection with August’s deadly hit-and-run incident on Patton Road. The pedestrian was struck while crossing the road in the early hours of the morning of Saturday, Aug 15th. The suspected driver initially fled the scene of the incident, but was tracked down and arrested. Read more.

MADISON

  1. Madison City High School students have had their return to the classroom delayed due to a spike in COVID-19 infections. With nine football players testing positive at Bob Jones, 120 students and ten teachers are in quarantine. Forty students and staffers at six other local schools are also quarantining. Madison City High Schools expects to resume in-person classes by Sept 28th. Read more.
