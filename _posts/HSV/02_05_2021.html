NORTH ALABAMA

  1. The John Hunt Park vaccination clinic will serve as one of eight mass-vaccination sites selected by the AL Dept of Public Health (ADPH). Each location will receive an extra 1,000 doses of vaccine to administer from Feb 8-12 to those in the expanded eligible category (healthcare workers, educators, 65-75, and essential workers). Appointments are required, and the site will not be drive-thru as originally stated. Eligibility is now open to residents outside of Madison Co, and the clinic will have extended hours from 7 am - 6 pm for the week of the blitz. Read more.

MADISON COUNTY

  1. The Madison Co. Health Department is halting new C-19 vaccinations due to a lack of vaccine supply. Residents who have received their first dose will get a second, but it may take a month or more before appointments become available for residents seeking their first dose. Appointments and waiting list reservations made through ADPH will not migrate to the vaccine blitz at John Hunt Park; those interested must sign up separately. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. Bank Independent and Prince Holdings broke ground Thursday on a new five-story office building in downtown HSV. The 77,000 square foot building will house a Bank Independent sales office, at least one restaurant, and retail and office space for lease. Construction is expected to finish within the next 15 - 18 months. Read more.

REDSTONE ARSENAL

  1. Redstone Arsenal is transitioning to Next Generation Uniformed Services ID cards. Plastic cards that incorporate additional security features will replace the old paper-based cards. Current cards remain valid until their listed expiration date. Those with indefinite expiration dates should wait until summer for new cards. Read more.

Spotlight

The 15th annual Melissa George Night with the Huntsville Havoc is coming up on Feb 13 at the VBC Propst Arena.

The fundraiser has raised nearly $670K for the Melissa George Neonatal Memorial Fund over the last 14 years. Money raised supports programs at the Regional Neonatal Intensive Care Unit (NICU) at the HSV Hospital for Women and Children. This year the NICU hopes to buy additional transcutaneous C02 monitors with the new funds. Attendance this year is limited due to C-19, but the jersey auction will be online and start Friday the 5th.
