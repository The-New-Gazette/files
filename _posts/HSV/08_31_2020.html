IMPORTANT

  1. As Madison County C-19 numbers fall, area public schools are preparing to re-open in September. Fall schedules in all districts will be staggered, divided by two groups determined by students’ last names. Students enrolled in virtual learning will continue to do so. All re-opening plans are dependent upon C-19 case numbers over the next two weeks.

    HSV City Schools will open earlier than planned, with pre-K through grade 8 returning the week of Sept. 14, and grades 9 thru 12 returning Sept. 21. Find the full staggered schedule here.

    Madison City Schools will stagger the return of Elementary, Middle, and High School students (September 8, 14, and 21, respectively). Read more.

    Madison County Schools will begin re-opening with a “transition” week Sept. 9 and traditional full weeks starting Sept. 21. Read more.

NORTH ALABAMA

  1. The Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC) fined TVA over $600K for workplace discrimination. An NRC investigation showed that two former TVA employees faced “adverse action” from management for revealing employees’ failure to report safety violations for fear of retaliation. Former TVA Vice President of Regulatory Affairs Joseph Shea has been prohibited from any NRC-licensed activities for five years, and a Notice of Violation was issued to former Director of Corporate Nuclear Licensing Erin Henderson. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. In compliance with the FAA, the HSV Intl Airport released a 2020 growth/expansion master plan. The Airport Authority, community leaders, and residents contributed to the planning phase for over three years, stressing the importance of terminal and concourse expansions. The blueprint also indicates that new parking garages and aircraft rescue and firefighter stations may be in the works. Read more.

    More: Read the full master plan here.

  2. Tut Fann Veteran Home employees, residents, and their families have reported concerns about the rising number of C-19 cases and deaths at the facility. Residents have been confined to their rooms since March with no family visitations. Seven resident deaths and two employee deaths were reported in the last two weeks, all of which are currently under review. The facility maintains that it has followed all Centers for Medicare and Medicaid guidelines for returning to work. Read more.

  3. $120K of a $1.2M donation from Boeing to fight racial injustice in Alabama will go to Girls, Inc. of Huntsville. The donation will support its Operation SMART program, which focuses on a STEM learning program aimed to reach over 700 girls in the HSV area. Read more.

    More: Read Boeing CEO Dave Calhoun’s full message on confronting systemic racism.

  4. The HSV Ballet Company (HBC) has canceled all 2020 performances due to C-19 concerns. For the first time in 51 years, “The Nutcracker” will not be performed in HSV. The HBC has launched a GoFundMe page to recoup lost revenue from canceled performances. Read more.

Spotlight

The HSV Museum of Art (HMA) has officially opened Hard Earned: The Military Photographs by Stacy Pearsall. Pearsall is a disabled Air Force veteran who has been shooting military photographs since the age of 17. She is one of only two women to have won the NPPA’s Military Photographer of the Year Award. Learn more about the exhibit.

The HMA is open to the public and following strict C-19 guidelines for social distancing and disinfecting during the pandemic. Learn about their precautions here.
