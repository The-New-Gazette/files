IMPORTANT

  1. HSV PD officer William Darby will go to trial today for the killing of Jeff Parker. Officer Darby fatally shot Mr. Parker in his own home after calling 911 in a mental health crisis three years ago. The HSV PD did not discipline Darby, but the DA’s office indicted him, and county prosecutors are charging him with murder. Activists plan to march at the MDS County Courthouse to demand Darby’s conviction and dismissal from the force. Read more.

STATEWIDE

  1. A new bill proposing the legalization of medical marijuana in AL has passed the state Senate and the House Health Committee. 23 of 42 district attorneys signed a letter arguing against its passage, citing several studies about the adverse effects of marijuana. The bill would not legalize recreational marijuana. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. HSV is celebrating its Historic Preservation Month by unveiling new sites as historical markers. Three new markers were announced commemorating the first woman elected to public office in Madison County, six Black women who claimed their right to vote, and Dr. Francis Roberts, a leader of HSV’s preservation movement. Current markers have seen few visitors and donations due to C-19, and The Historic HSV Foundation hopes this month’s activities will get things rolling again. Read more.

REDSTONE ARSENAL

  1. The Army Vice Chief of Staff visited Redstone Arsenal to see the developing technologies and discuss new protocols for crime investigations. The top priority of the visit was to review and inspect new technologies in person. While at the base, he also discussed the recently introduced Military Justice Improvement and Increasing Prevention Act, which shifts investigations from the chain of command to independent prosecutors for various crimes, including sexual assault. Read more.
