MADISON COUNTY

  1. The HSV-Madison County Public Library is planning to launch Blast, a free music streaming service featuring local artists. The library will accept submissions from local musicians until June 30. The streaming platform, which will serve as documented music collection for the North Alabama area, will debut this summer. Read more.

  2. There has been an update in the trial of HSV police officer William Darby. Madison County Circuit Judge Donna Pate rejected the defense’s motion to exclude a statement from another officer, allowing the jury to hear that testimony. Officer Darby faces a murder charge for the April 2018 on-duty shooting at the home of Jeffery Parker. Read more.

  1. An HSV woman has been accused of faking a terminal cancer diagnosis in order to deceive the community. She manipulated local individuals and churches into donating thousands of dollars. MADISON CITY

  1. Madison City Schools developed a three-pronged approach to help students who have fallen behind in school due to the C-19 pandemic. The summer learning programs are designed for students who have experienced gaps or loss in learning and are funded by the Elementary and Secondary School Emergency Relief Fund. Classes will be held between June 7 and July 2, focusing on English and math standards. Read more.
