IMPORTANT

  1. A jury found Officer William Darby of the HSV PD guilty of the murder of Jeffrey Parker. Darby fatally shot Parker in 2018 after responding to a 911 mental health crisis call. Darby’s attorneys argued that his actions were in defense of his fellow officers, but the prosecutors maintained that Darby escalated the situation. Read more.

    More: Read responses to the verdict from the Parker family, Police Chief McMurray, and Mayor Battle here.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. Amazon announced it will build a new fulfillment center in the HSV metro area near Limestone Co. The one million sq. ft. facility, which is expected to create 500 new jobs in North Alabama, is slated to open in the fall of 2021. Employees at the center will pack and ship large items such as furniture and rugs. Read more.

  2. HSV city officials are seeking federal grant money to help pay for a pedestrian sky bridge over Memorial Pkwy. The project aims to link the east and west parts of the city and connect the Lowe Mill area to the downtown core. The HSV City Council voted to approve a proposal to hire The Ferguson Group to write a grant proposal to fund half of the $50M project. Read more.

  3. The University of Alabama System (HSV, Birmingham, Tuscaloosa) announced that vaccinated students and faculty no longer need to wear masks in most situations. Chancellor Finis St. John stated he is “confident we will have a full, normal, on-campus experience” for the Fall 2021 semester. Read more.

  4. Madison City Schools announced that June 1 will mark the end of mask requirements for students, faculty, and staff. The school system’s Spring semester ends May 25; this policy update will begin with Summer classes. School board leaders cited low rates of new C-19 infections in the school district as the basis for their decision. Read more.

  5. UAH announced Wednesday that it is suspending the Charger hockey program after the team failed to secure a conference affiliation. The Central Collegiate Hockey Association did not accept UAH’s proposal, and the Atlantic Hockey Association has not yet responded. UAH has stated it will reinstate the program if the Chargers secure a conference membership, but the team will not be eligible to play for at least a year after the invitation. Read more.

CITY OF MADISON

  1. The City of Madison has selected Captain Johnny Gandy of the Virginia Beach Police Department as Madison’s next police chief. City Council passed a resolution to enter into negotiations with the candidate, and the council will vote on employment terms and make the official appointment at its June 14 meeting. Read more.

QUICK BITS

  • Update: The VBC reports that an April collapse at the Propst Arena may have been caused by the failure of a contractor to follow architectural plans. All ceiling components installed at the time of those which failed will be replaced. Read more.
  • HSV Hospital will discontinue its daily posting of C-19 numbers next week and begin posting weekly on June 2. Read more.
  • Redstone Arsenal will host a free, public party on June 12 to celebrate the U.S. Army’s 246th birthday. Events will include a car show, food trucks, craft & farm vendors, a fun run, and fireworks. Read more.
  • New airline Breeze Airways has chosen Huntsville as one of its first cities. Breeze will offer nonstop flights to New Orleans, Charleston, and Tampa. Read more.
