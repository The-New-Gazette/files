MADISON CO.

  1. Madison Co. has delayed all capital murder cases until at least 2021, out of concern for juror safety. Citing social-distancing concerns, the presiding judge, Ruth Ann Hall, said the courthouse in HSV doesn’t have enough space to allow for jury selection in capital cases safely. At least three capital murder cases were set to be tried this year. Read more.

    More: Trials in other types of cases are set to resume next month on Oct 19th.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. Northrop Grumman (“NG”) was awarded a $13.3 B contract developing new ground-based intercontinental ballistic missiles. The new missiles will replace the current Minuteman III ICBMs, which were developed by Boeing and first became operational in 1970. The Northrop Grumman team will comprise several leading companies from across the defense, engineering, and construction industries, many of whom are located here in HSV. Read more.

  2. Two local long-term care facilities have reported an alarming increase in C-19 infections in recent weeks.

    Tut Fann Veterans’ Home now has 50 residents who have been infected after an additional ten people tested positive within the last week. Of the 50 infected residents, seven have died, and six have recovered. Nearly 30 employees at the facility have also tested positive. Read more.

    Signature HealthCARE of Whitesburg Gardens had 25 residents and eight employees who tested positive as of last week. Since the pandemic began, 75 residents have been infected, nine have died (4 were already in hospice care), and 52 have recovered. Twenty-eight employees at the facility have also tested positive. Read more.

MADISON

  1. Madison City middle schools will re-open as scheduled on Sept 14th, despite a recent increase in C-19 infections. Superintendent, Dr. Ed Nichols, has sought to reassure parents that the right procedures and protocols are in place to protect students as in-person learning is phased in. Madison City’s elementary schools reopened yesterday, with middle schools returning on Sept 14th, and high schools on Sept 21st. Read more.

    Context: While schools are re-opening, the Alabama Department of Public Health raised the Madison Co. infection rate from yellow to red on Friday.

  2. Dr. Kerry Donaldson has been named as the new administrator of The Academy for Madison City Schools. Currently, the assistant principal at James Clemens High School, Dr. Donaldson, replaces Kevin Wilson, who has retired. Having spent 17 years in the field of education, this is his 3rd year in Madison. Read more.

Spotlight

This weekend sees the return of the Monte Sano Arts Festival! First held in 1999, the festival began as a locally-organized art show with around 30 artists participating. Since its inception, it has rapidly expanded to become a two-day show, with over 165 artists participating. Set against the backdrop of the glorious autumn colors at Monte Sano State Park, the Festival is one of the mainstays of HSV’s fall events calendar.

This year, due to the C-19 and social-distancing, the festival will be a scaled-down version of itself, with approximately 100 local and regional artists in attendance, and a selection of local food trucks.
