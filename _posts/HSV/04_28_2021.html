STATEWIDE

  1. To commemorate the 10th anniversary of the April 27, 2011 tornado disasters, Gov. Ivey announced that April 27th will be a statewide “Day of Remembrance.” Flags were flown at half staff to honor the 254 lives lost and more than 2,200 injured by the deadly storms. Read more.

    More: Longtime HSV meteorologist Gary Dobbs gives a detailed account of his 2011 Lawrence County home tornado experience. Read more.

NORTH ALABAMA

  1. In Florence, the Southern Poverty Law Center has begun a billboard campaign to oppose this past Monday’s state holiday, Confederate Memorial Day. The SPLC is calling to move Confederate monuments and statues to cemeteries, out of public spaces. The first billboard, bringing attention to the Lauderdale County Courthouse monument, is located on Hwy 72. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. The Ledges is hosting the HSV Championship PGA Korn Ferry Tour, with tournament rounds taking place Thursday, April 29 - Sunday, May 2. This is the final competition to determine PGA Tour eligibility. Tickets are available online, with spectator parking located at the Sandra Moon Community Complex. Read more.

    More: Professional golfer and Ardmore native Lee Hodges will return to compete in the Tour event. Read more.

  2. Hatch, a workforce development nonprofit organization, is launching a new 8-week program combining life skills with hospitality and culinary training to create a stronger local hospitality workforce. The program will be available to people in the Huntsville area between 18-24 years old and will pay each student $1,250. Read more.
