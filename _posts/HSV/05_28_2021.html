HUNTSVILLE

  1. HSV city officials are seeking federal grant money to help pay for a pedestrian skybridge over Memorial Pkwy. The project aims to link the east and west parts of the city and connect the Lowe Mill area to the downtown core. Last night, the city council voted to approve a proposal to hire The Ferguson Group to write a grant proposal to fund half of the $50M project.  Read more.

  2. The City of HSV warns residents of possible alligators in southern and western areas of town. Sightings have increased recently near Haysland Road. Wildlife experts advise the public to observe alligators and wildlife from a safe distance and refrain from interacting with wild animals. Reports of alligator sightings at the Tennessee River date back to 1894. Read more.

  3. At last night’s City Council meeting, city administrator John Hamilton proposed new changes to the remodeling of Joe Davis Stadium. The stadium is currently undergoing renovation to accommodate local high school football. New changes would include varied seating and an expanded food and beverage service to draw a local professional soccer team. Should new sports opportunities not work out, Mayor Battle explained that the city could revert to the original remodeling plan. Read more.

Spotlight

Owner and chef Albert Toh brings a whole new continent of toppings to the humble hot dog at his food truck, New South Hotdog & Sushi. Toh moved to HSV from his native Malaysia 15 years ago to attend college. Two years ago, he decided to leave his 9-to-5 job at a university to get creative with hot dogs. Menu items include Crunch Dogs, Volcano Dogs, and Mojito Dogs, as well as sushi rolls. Toh points to the multicultural cuisine of his childhood in Borneo as inspiration for the diverse ingredients he uses. His many college jobs in restaurants triggered his interest in sushi. Read more.
