NORTH ALABAMA

  1. Last week’s inclement weather continues to delay vaccination efforts in some North AL communities. Colbert and Lauderdale county health departments opened over the weekend to catch up, but Helen Keller Hospital in Sheffield has had to reschedule second-dose appointments for next week. The hospital will not accept new appointments for first doses until a new shipment arrives. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. HSV Hospital and Oakwood University signed the lease Wednesday for the Community Action Center in northwest HSV. The new clinic will provide check-ups and triage care for underserved community members and education and internships for future healthcare providers. The clinic is under construction and will open this summer. Read more.

  2. A research team led by Greg Cooper, Ph.D., of HudsonAlpha, identified a link between the RALA gene and some developmental disabilities. Using a tool called GeneMatcher, researchers worldwide contributed to discovering that variations on the gene link to a set of developmental delays and intellectual disabilities that include speech problems, muscular issues, and dysmorphic facial features. The discovery may lead to earlier diagnoses and better support services for those affected. Read more.

  3. HSV police intervened in an attempted robbery at Target in Jones Valley yesterday. After being confronted by police in the parking lot, the suspect sped away, dragging the officer a short distance before crashing into a parked car. The suspect is now in custody and faces multiple charges, including third-degree theft and second-degree assault. The officer is recovering from road rash and minor injuries. Read more.

CITY OF MADISON

  1. Madison City Council unanimously voted to form a Police Citizens Advisory Committee to promote dialogue between the community and law enforcement. The nine-member committee will serve in an advisory capacity to the city police department on officer training, quality of life issues, and community concerns. Council members, Mayor Paul Finley, and the Chief of Police will appoint committee members and are currently accepting applications for the positions. Read more.

    More: Committee members must be at least 18 years old, reside in Madison, and be willing to serve for two to four years. Apply here.

  2. Some students who attend Madison City Elementary School will be zoned to a new school next year. Construction is underway on Midtown Elementary School off of Wall Triana Hwy near Kroger. The new school will open in August and serve around 840 students. Zoning changes were announced Tuesday, and the school district is accepting feedback through Sunday. Read more.
