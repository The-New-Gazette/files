  1. HSV City council members questioned the police department’s investigation procedures in reaction to the fatal shooting of Mr. Rivas on Oct. 16. NORTH ALABAMA

  1. Boeing has given Decatur City Schools a $50,000 grant to prepare students for STEM careers. The grant will provide a composite lab for the Engineering Academy, a 3D printer for 7th and 8th graders, and expand a Greenpower USA program that teaches 4th – 6th graders how to build their own electric cars. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. A panel including Mayor Tommy Battle and other city leaders discussed HSV’s new growth strategy. The plan emphasizes infrastructure, high quality of life, good jobs, and the strategic placement of new company locations in anticipation of the growth HSV will see from the FBI and other large organizations. Read more.

  2. HSV is named the “most affordable place to live in the U.S.” for the third consecutive year. The city’s above-average individual annual salary and the low cost of living land it at #1 out of the 150 most heavily populated metro areas in the country. Read more.

  3. DSV, a local cargo transport company, and the Port of Huntsville are preparing for C-19 vaccine distribution. DSV says HSV International Airport is a great location for distribution because it is less congested than other venues and has 26,000 sqft of temperature-controlled coolers. They hope this positions them well to compete for a role in the distribution of the future vaccine. Read more.

MADISON

  1. A tanker overturned in a ditch near the intersection of AL-53 and Harvest Road late Saturday night. Madison County Fire closed the road through the night and most of Sunday to safely clean the oil spill and remove the tanker. Read more.
