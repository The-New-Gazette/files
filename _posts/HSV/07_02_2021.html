News

HUNTSVILLE

  1. Christopher Henderson has been found guilty on all murder counts, including the murders of three children under the age of 14. The murders occurred at a New Market residence in 2015, which Henderson then set on fire. His defense attorney blamed his ex-wife, to whom he was still married at the time of the murders. However, footage of Henderson was found on his phone practicing for the murders in the weeks beforehand, indicating premeditation. Read more.

  2. Repairs on Church St. may be to blame for the deaths of several fish in the adjacent Big Spring Park pond. Water was pumped away from the culvert under Church St., diverting currents toward the International Park. Park visitors reported several dead fish in the main lagoon on Sunday, after which city officials responded multiple times to ensure the safety of the Big Spring koi fish and conditions of the pond water. Read more.

  3. City Council member Devyn Keith sat down with WHNT earlier this week for an in-depth discussion about possible improvements to local police procedures. Keith believes recent interactions between the HPD and Black Lives Matter protestors and Black citizens are negatively affecting HSV’s image in the national spotlight. He suggests a few policy changes for the future as HSV quickly grows. Watch the full interview here.

  4. Local blood shortages continue as elective surgeries resume and people seek medical care for issues they did not treat during the pandemic. A national blood shortage has directly affected HSV & North AL’s LifeSouth blood provider, causing the organization to seek donations urgently. People who received any of the three C-19 vaccines are eligible to give blood if they are feeling well when they donate. Read more.

    Action: Make an appointment with LifeSouth to donate here.
