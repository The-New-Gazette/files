News

HUNTSVILLE

  1. The murder trial for Christopher Henderson, the Madison County man, began last week with graphic details of the multiple homicides of his family members in 2015. Henderson’s second wife, and co-conspirator, testified against him on Friday in an apparent plea deal to avoid capital punishment, along with multiple investigators and the medical examiner. Discretion advised as the sourced article contains disturbing details. Read more.

  2. Former HSV doctor Marshall Plotka pleaded guilty on Thursday to one count of maintaining a drug-involved premise as part of a plea deal with federal prosecutors. Plotka stood accused of storing drugs at a home in the Owens Cross Roads area and supplying them to women hired as prostitutes. He was arrested in 2019, and his trial is scheduled to begin this month. Read more.

  3. The new Toyota Corolla Cross SUV will be the first vehicle manufactured at the HSV Mazda Toyota plant. The front-wheel-drive SUV gets an average of 36.2 miles per gallon. Production begins later this year; vehicles will are expected to be in dealer lots by Fall. The plant is still hiring and hopes to employ up to 4K people in 2021. Read more.

  4. An HSV police officer will face disciplinary action for stomping multiple times on a man’s leg while attempting to arrest him. The man who was arrested is facing charges for resisting arrest, and his attorney announced plans to sue the city. His mother says he has schizophrenia which marks another local instance of excessive police force towards someone with a mental health issue. Read more.

  5. HSV City Council approved the first step for a new $325M mixed-use development across from the VBC on Clinton Ave. The 13-acre site, which was once the downtown site of the Coca-Cola bottling company, is poised to become new downtown residences, commercial spaces, and a hotel. The NYC-based developers have stated that they are considering the proximity to the VBC in their designs. More details will become available as the project proceeds. Read more.

  6. Acting Air Force Secretary John Roth confirmed that no political forces influenced the decision to move Space Command headquarters to HSV. In a Congressional hearing on Wednesday, Roth testified that, despite recent upgrades to the current facilities in Colorado, Alabama’s lower construction and maintenance costs led the Air Force to choose HSV. Read more.

REDSTONE ARSENAL

  1. The Biden administration’s proposed $24.8B NASA budget for 2022 keeps Marshall Space Flight Center central in the U.S.’s return to the Moon. A combination of legacy space companies like SpaceX and Boeing will continue to work on an updated SLS. The new budget also introduces an emphasis on Earth observation via a new platform of satellites. Read more.

  2. A groundbreaking ceremony was held for the new $1.3B FBI Innovation Center at Redstone Arsenal. The campus will focus on intelligence, cyber threats, and analytics, house around 4-5 thousand jobs, and include a kinetic cyber range and VR classrooms for agent training simulations. Construction on the initial phase of the 9 building campus is expected to be complete in 2023. Read more.

    More: See the planned campus overview here.

  3. The Marshall Space Flight Center Headquarters Building, known to many as 4200, is set to be demolished and replaced by 2030. The 58-year-old building has mechanical and structural issues amounting to $1M in annual maintenance and operations costs. One particular issue among many prompting the decision is parts of the external facade dropping off the building creating safety concerns. Read more.

    More: The 4200 building has had a long and prestigious role in the history of the U.S. Space Program. You can read more here.
