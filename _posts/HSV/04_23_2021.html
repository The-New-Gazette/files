News

IMPORTANT

  1. The HSV Citizens Advisory Council shared their findings on HSV PD’s response to last summer’s downtown racial protests with HSV City Council. The findings shared that officers did violate department policy in some instances, including inappropriate use of chemical agents, rubber bullets, and bean bag munitions. The report expressed a critical need for more training in response to protests against police violence and racial issues. The 248-page report can be read here, and you can watch the proceedings here.

    More: HSV PD was the only agency that participated with the advisory council’s review, even though multiple agencies, including the Madison PD and Madison County Sheriff’s Office, were present during the protests. No officers were disciplined for their actions during the protest.

NORTH ALABAMA

  1. The Huntsville Hospital system is offering a $5K bonus for experienced full-time Registered Nurse hires. Different positions are open at different locations. The upgraded pay scale also includes a $3K signing bonus for new graduates. The system is offering bonuses to compete in a high-demand market. According to the AL Department of Labor, hiring ads for RNs have increased 65% since a year ago. Read more.

  2. Five defendants were indicted this week on three counts of capital murder in the fatal July shooting of a Hartselle man in his home. Anthony Larry Sheppard was found dead at home by police conducting a welfare check when he failed to appear in court on July 24. The mother of Sheppard’s child, Jaclyn Skuce of Madison, used a fake Facebook account to find and hire Logan McKinley Delp to arrange the killing for $90K. The other defendants served as lookout, support, and getaway driver. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. Police have arrested 18-year-old Vincent Harmon for the reckless murder of Seth Anderson following a shooting on Monday, April 19. 20-year-old Anderson died of gunshot wounds at HSV Hospital Monday night. He was the son of Amy Bishop Anderson, who is currently serving a life sentence in prison for the murder of three UAH faculty members in 2010. Read more.

CITY OF MADISON

  1. The Rocket City Trash Pandas President and CEO has resigned and confirmed the organization will not pay the City of Madison the full $1M guarantee. CEO Ralph Nelson says he is resigning to move onto new ventures and that the team lost a projected $17.8M in revenue last year due to the pandemic. The team played no games but laid off no employees; the stadium made $1.79M from 175 other events and $4M through merchandising. Read more.

    More: According to statements from the City and Mayor Paul Finley, the city is finalizing the 2020 season audits and has already received over $165K from Ballcorps. See the statements.
