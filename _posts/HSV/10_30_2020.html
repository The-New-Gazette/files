MADISON COUNTY

  1. HSV/Madison Co. Chamber of Commerce formed an official partnership with the Rocket City Chapter of Federation for Advanced Manufacturing Education. The workforce education program trains highly skilled talent to meet manufacturing companies' unique needs and serves as a training and recruitment tool for Toyota. One goal of the partnership is to expand education to additional manufacturing sectors. Read more.

  2. Roger Jones is retiring after 20 years of serving as Madison Co. District One Commissioner. Tom Brandon was elected as the next Commissioner; he plans to complete several unfinished projects, including the Winchester Rd expansion and the bridge on Carragher Rd. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. Gov. Ivey announced that WellStone Behavioral Health in HSV is one of three mental health clinics in AL receiving funds to expand help to people with mental illnesses. To provide the specific care they need, people in a mental health crisis will be taken to a crisis center instead of emergency rooms or jails. Read more.

  2. Health Connect America is seeking applicants for foster parents to provide safe, caring homes. C-19 has limited the agency’s usual methods of recruiting new foster families. There are currently over 6,000 children in the AL foster care system. Read more.

Spotlight

The Huntsville Ballet won nonprofit of the year for the first time at the 35th Annual Small Business Awards. The Ballet has been facing financial struggles due to C-19 and has had to cancel performances this year. The nonprofit is fundraising to pay their rent, mortgage, and professional dancers and has found a benefactor to match donations if $25K can be raised by Dec 15th. Read more.
