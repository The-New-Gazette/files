NORTH ALABAMA

  1. The C-19 variant from the UK was found in AL last week. This strain is more infectious, and health officials recommend doubling-down on current precautions, including buying a mask with multiple layers or wearing two masks at once. Read more.

  2. Doug Jones’ career continues as a CNN political commentator. After losing his Senate seat to Tommy Tuberville earlier this year, Jones has joined as a commentator on CNN’s show The Lead with his debut covering President Biden’s proposed C-19 relief bill this past week. He was also named as a politics fellow at Georgetown University.

MADISON COUNTY

  1. C-19 vaccine access expands to 65+ on February 8th. However, local hospitals will need time to expand distribution. Officials say it may be weeks before the newly eligible phase 1b group can be vaccinated. About 8k people, requiring 16k doses, still need to receive the vaccination across the county. HSV Hospital plans to deliver 500 first round and 500 second-round vaccines each day. Read more.

    More: If you are eligible, you can register with HSV Hospital.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. Senators and Representatives from New Mexico are piling on to the demand to review the selection of HSV as Space Command’s new HQ. They have echoed the same concerns of political favoritism and impropriety in the process as voiced by the Colorado delegation. A key red-flag was that the decision was handed down on President Trump’s last day in office. Read more.

    Context: You can read the full letter from the New Mexico delegation here (pdf).

    More: The Secretary of Defense, Lloyd Austin, has not released a statement on reviewing the decision. However, there is a report that verbal intent to review was given to Sen. Martin Heinrich of New Mexico. Please note, we cannot confirm this claim made in the Albuquerque Journal from any other source at this time.

Spotlight

The second annual MidCity Mardi Gras is up and running until Feb. 16, featuring Cajun food such as gumbo, crawfish fries, and king cake bread pudding. A pop-up bar will serve various NOLA-inspired drinks (some straight out of Bourbon Street), and the grand finale will be an outdoor concert on the 13th.

The event will be open Tuesdays through Thursdays from 4–9 pm, Fridays 4–10 pm, Saturdays 11 am –10 pm, and Sunday 11 am – 8 pm. Masks are required, and all events will observe C-19 safety.
