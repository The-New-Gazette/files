IMPORTANT

  1. After 42 years in office, AL Sen. Richard Shelby announced he would not seek re-election in 2022. Shelby is the longest-serving Alabamian in the U.S. Senate and the current vice chairman of the Senate Appropriations Committee. He has served as chairman of the Senate Committee on Rules and Administration and the Senate Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs Committee. In Shelby’s retirement, Alabama loses a senior ranking Senator who has fueled a large amount of defense spending to the state. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. Blossomwood residents expressed concern at the recent HSV City Schools Board meeting about the Annie Mertz Center's upcoming relocation to a former church site on McClung Ave. The residents brought up issues of traffic and safety at what they claim is already a dangerous intersection. HSV City Schools can no longer afford the upkeep on its current aging building downtown, which prompted the additional property purchase. Read more.

  2. AL AG Steve Marshall announced that the state is receiving $9,229,421 in a landmark multi-state settlement with McKinsey & Company for its harmful promotion of opioids as pain reducers. Alabama has the highest opioid prescription rate in the nation and resulted in many opioid-related deaths. The City of Huntsville is seeking a portion of those funds and has filed its own lawsuit against McKinsey & Company in 2019. Read more.

    Context: In 2019, three separate Madison County doctors were charged for knowingly prescribing opioids to drug addicts, for recruiting sex workers to become patients, and signing blank prescription forms for staffers to fill out. Read more.

  3. The additional 5000 C-19 vaccine doses that HSV Hospital received were distributed this week, and capacity will return to 1,000 doses per day after Friday. VP of Operations Tracy Doughty shared that C-19 hospitalizations have halved since last month and stressed the continued importance of following CDC guidelines as new virus variants spread. Read more.
