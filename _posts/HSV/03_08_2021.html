News

HUNTSVILLE

  1. UAH will collaborate in the launch of a program providing people with visual impairments with access to information through tactile mediums. The program, called Accessible Graphics Consortium, will train educators in tactile graphics. The partnership has gained traction as very little visual content in the public sphere is accessible to people who are blind and visually impaired. Read more.

  2. HSV Hospital’s second C-19 vaccine blitz distributed 9,000 doses last week. Starting today, the clinic will return to its previous capacity of delivering 2,500 doses per week. If the hospital receives additional Johnson & Johnson vaccines, the capacity may increase. Read more.

  3. The Veterans Affairs Health Care System is now accepting vaccine appointments for eligible veterans 40 years and older. Eligible veterans are those who are already enrolled or qualified to enroll in the VA system. Appointments can be made by calling this number: (256) 533-8477. Read more.

  4. Police have arrested suspect Shamekia Strickland related to the killing of Jessie Suttle this past Wednesday. Mr. Suttle died from multiple injuries after a physical altercation with Ms. Strickland, now charged with capital murder. Read more.

Spotlight

In an effort to adhere to safety protocols, Girl Scouts troops will sell their cookies via pop-up booths. Payment forms will be contactless, and the cookies will be given to the customers on trays rather than by hand to avoid physical contact. Madison County’s first pop-up will be Saturday from 10 am to 5 pm outside of the old Design World building in Madison at 8059 US-72. You can also order cookies through the GrubHub app to have them delivered directly to you. Read more.
