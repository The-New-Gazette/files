NORTH ALABAMA

  1. The President has removed James Thompson, an Alabamian, and the board chairman of the Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA). The removal of Mr. Thompson and another board member was in response to the TVA's recent plan to outsource local tech jobs overseas. The President also critiqued TVA's CEO, Jeff Lyash, calling for his removal and claiming that Lyash is overpaid and underperforming. Read more.

    Context: TVA is a federal corporation like AMTRAK and the U.S. Postal Service, meaning the government has direct oversight while the corporation offers a market-oriented product or service.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. The U.S. Space and Rocket Center (USSRC) has reached its fundraising goal of $1.5 million to remain open. Facing a critical shortfall in visitors and donations as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic, the USSRC had recently announced that it would not be able to continue operations past October. With nationwide support, the funds were raised in less than a week. Read more.

  2. HSV Police Department (HPD) is receiving a dedicated training facility and a new firing range. The HSV City Council has allocated $10.2 million for a purpose-built facility housing both police and fire training along Triana Boulevard. HPD will also receive an additional $3 million to develop a new 50-acre firing range in partnership with the FBI. Read more.

  3. The HSV City Council (HCC) remains optimistic about the removal of the Confederate monument from the courthouse square. A 2017 state law currently stymies plans for moving the memorial. A bill has been pre-filed for the upcoming state senate session to override the 2017 law returning control over historical monuments and structures to local officials. Read more.

    Context: The HSV City Council, along with the County Commission, which has jurisdiction over the monument, have both passed resolutions calling for its removal to city-owned Maple Hill Cemetery.

  4. Mayor Battle says HSV's multi-million dollar investment in Remington Arms is protected.  As Remington goes through bankruptcy again, HSV's $12.5 M investment will be recouped as the facility, appraised at $47.3 M, will revert to the city. The immediate concern for city officials is safeguarding the jobs of the employees at the plant. The hope is for another tenant, preferably another gun-maker, to move into the building as soon as possible. Read more.

If you enjoy the outdoors and are seeking new horizons, or looking for inspiration for your next hike, check out the 100 Alabama Miles Challenge. It encourages Alabamians to kickstart a healthier lifestyle by getting active outdoors and discovering new places.
