News

STATEWIDE

  1. Three-term Madison County Commission Chairman Dale Strong announced intentions to run for the 5th Congressional District. Strong is pursuing Rep. Mo Brooks’ seat in the U.S. Congress, which will only be vacant if Brooks’ run for the U.S. Senate succeeds. Strong is attempting to follow Brooks’ path from Madison County Commission to the U.S. Congress. Read more.

    More: Meanwhile, Rep. Brooks has announced his candidacy for the U.S. Senate. Read more.

MADISON COUNTY

  1. Presiding Judge Ruth Ann Hall is pushing state legislators for additional circuit judgeships for Madison County as the area population grows. Only one new circuit position has been added since 1974, bringing the total to seven. For context, Jefferson County (Birmingham), has 27 circuit judges. Huntsville-area Senator Sam Givhan is a sponsor of Senate Bill 286, which provides additional judgeships for four judicial circuits including Madison County. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. The Panoply Arts Festival will return this year with new C-19 safety precautions. This year’s interpretation will be called “A Taste of Panoply” and will give the community an opportunity to support local artists and musicians while remaining socially distanced and safe. It is scheduled for April 23–25 at Big Spring Park downtown. Read more.

MADISON

  1. A groundbreaking was held on Tuesday for Journey Middle School, a new addition to the Madison City School district. The 170,000 sq.ft. facility with an estimated cost of $51M is on track to open in fall 2022 for approximately 1,200 students. Read more.

  2. Gov. Kay Ivey directed ALDOT to expedite the Town Madison exit ramp opening after receiving a letter from Madison Mayor Paul Finley for help on the matter. ALDOT is currently working on completing the highway interchange and estimates the construction will take a few weeks to complete. Read more.
