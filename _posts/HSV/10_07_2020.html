IMPORTANT

  1. John Meredith won the runoff election for HSV City Council District 5. After a close race in August with no clear winner, Mr. Meredith has overcome three-time incumbent Will Culver by capturing 61% of the runoff vote yesterday. John Meredith is a former Capitol Hill lobbyist and the son of the famous civil rights leader James Meredith, known for being the first black student at the University of Mississippi. Read more.

    Extra: Mr. Meredith ran on a 5-point platform for West HSV.

MADISON COUNTY

  1. A cottage-community is being built at Davidson Farms to house at-risk girls aging out of foster care. The first of its kind, the community of five tiny homes are being constructed in Ardmore by Kids to Love. The goal is to provide a ‘bridge to independence’ for the young women, supporting them as they transition into adulthood, while still allowing them to remain under the guidance of the Kids to Love team of mentors. Read more.

  2. HSV and Madison police are seeking a murder suspect after a death investigation off Slaughter Road on Tuesday. The two police departments in a joint investigation said they are actively looking for Johnathan Taylor Stephens, 39, who is wanted for the murder of Michael Anthony Baker. Stephens is 6’ 2”, 205 pounds, and has brown hair and brown eyes. He should be considered armed and dangerous. Read more.

    More: If you have any information on Stephens’ whereabouts, call HSV PD’s tip line at (256) 532-7463.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. HSV City Schools will be expanding in-person classes to five days a week starting October 26th. The decision to expand from the current 2 or 3 days a week schedule was made based on the reduced number of C-19 cases in the district. Additional face and desk shields have been ordered in anticipation of the change. Read more.

  2. The number of C-19 in-patients at HSV Hospital is at their lowest point since summer began. There are 39 patients housed at the main campus, 9 on ventilators, and 11 in intensive care. At the end of July, the hospital had more than one hundred C-19 in-patient cases. Read more.

    More: For detailed reports on C-19 trends in AL and Madison County, see Bamatracker.com.

  3. Local HSV OB-GYN, Dr. John Cimino, has been sentenced after being found guilty of fraud. Dr. Cimino was caught up in a wave of federal authorities' arrests in April last year and subsequently pled guilty to defrauding TRICARE. He has been sentenced to 3-years probation with 8-months home confinement and must pay almost $245K in restitution. Read more.
