NORTH ALABAMA

  1. The Alabama Department of Public Health (ADPH) is changing quarantine guidelines for schools. Close contacts will now only be sent home to quarantine if the person they have been exposed to has tested positive for coronavirus or has lost taste or smell. Read more.

    Extra: View the updated ADPH Back to School Toolkit here.

  2. North AL Seniors and Medicare users will have additional health insurance options in 2021. VIVA HEALTH is partnering with HSV Hospital and UAB health systems to provide three new Medicare Advantage plans in Limestone, Madison, and Morgan counties. Medicare’s annual open enrollment period is Oct. 15 - Dec. 7. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. HSV Mayor Tommy Battle delivered the 2020 State of the City Address on Wednesday. Despite setbacks from the C-19 pandemic, HSV has seen the creation of nearly 1,000 new jobs and significant new construction and commercial investments. 2021 will bring more road work, Trader Joe’s, and 2 Gb Internet service from Google Fiber. Read more.

    More: Watch full the address here.

  2. UAH President Darren Dawson released an apology Wednesday in response to allegations of inappropriate behavior by a campus police officer during a traffic stop on Oct. 3. The mother of the driver, a 17-year-old Black male UAH student, posted a detailed response to the incident on social media, sparking a response from the university. According to the President’s statement, the UAH police department will undergo additional training and review current policies and procedures. Read more.

    More: Read the official statement.

  3. Drake State Community & Technical College has won Marshall Space Flight Center’s Cooperative Agreement Notice (CAN) award and is the first historically Black community college to do so. Students and instructors from Drake’s Engineering Design program will test 3D printed structures for future construction projects on the Moon. Read more.
