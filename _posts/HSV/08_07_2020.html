NORTH ALABAMA

  1. A virtual candidate forum is being held next week for the August 25 HSV and Madison municipal elections. Mayoral candidates for both cities will speak on August 11, and city council candidates will speak on August 12. Register here.

    Related: Here is essential election information for the City of Madison and HSV.

  2. Eight North Alabama nursing homes will receive rapid COVID-19 testing machines from the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services. Facilities in Madison, Limestone, Colbert, Lauderdale, Marshall, and Madison counties should receive the devices within the next two weeks. The machines have been sent to prioritized locations to help curb viral spread with rapid and more cost-effective testing. Read more.

MADISON CO.

  1. Madison Co. leaders shared promising numbers in Wednesday’s COVID-19 update but warned that healthcare workers are still struggling with current caseloads. New cases, testing volume, and the number of people in quarantine have all declined over the last two weeks. Home quarantines have dropped 28 percent, and testing volume has reduced 21 percent. Read more.

    Related: According to HSV Hospital CEO David Spillers, there are currently 202 inpatients with coronavirus systemwide. 156 Madison Co. employees are out for issues related to the virus. The burden on staff has led to delays in some elective procedures. Read more.

  2. Madison Co. Sheriff’s Office is investigating the vandalism of the Confederate monument in downtown HSV after it was defaced with red paint early on Wednesday. The state law that blocks removal of the statue does allow for monuments to be moved for emergency repairs, but County Chairman Dale Strong states that there are currently no plans for cleaning or repairs. Read more.

    More: Some protestors gathered Wednesday to say “Let the Stain Remain,” arguing that tax dollars should not be spent to clean the statue. Read more.
