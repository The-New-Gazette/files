STATEWIDE

  1. The AL National Guard will deploy later this month to aid vaccination efforts across the state. Mobilized units will travel to counties in the Black Belt region and other underserved communities, with the plan to vaccinate up to 4K people each week. Read more.

  2. The National Governors Association awarded AL a $100K grant to help develop programs for workers displaced by the pandemic. The funds will support the state’s Skills-Based Recovery Initiative to provide training and support services for workers, technical assistance for employers, and gap analysis for trainers to make sure programs create viable pathways to post-pandemic employment. Read more.

MADISON COUNTY

  1. Across the county, C-19 hospitalizations are the lowest they’ve been since October. HSV Hospital officials said increased vaccinations are helping to keep those most at risk out of the hospital. The hospital is again receiving 5K doses a week for distribution, including some Johnson & Johnson single-dose vaccines. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. The HSV City Council passed the final requirement to open a C-19 rental assistance program. To be eligible, a household must have income at or below 80% of the Area Median Income (AMI) and meet additional criteria, including showing a loss of income due to C-19 or demonstrating current unsafe living conditions. The online portal for rental assistance opens on Monday, March 15th, at 8 am. Read more.

REDSTONE ARSENAL

  1. Facial recognition lanes open at Redstone Arsenal on March 15. The pilot program uses cameras and facial recognition software to grant access to personnel currently registered in the Automated Installation Entry System. Cameras at Gate 1 (lane 3) and Gate 9 (lanes 3 and 4) will scan passengers' facial features in single-occupancy vehicles. Guards will be in-lane the first four business days to assist with initial entry. Read more.

Spotlight

Hays Nature Preserve in Owens Cross Roads offers outdoor activities for every family member, including the dogs!
