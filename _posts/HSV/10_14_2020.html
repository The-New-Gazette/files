NORTH ALABAMA

  1. The U.S. District of Court of Appeals for the 11th Circuit has overruled a lower court decision related to the upcoming general election. Judge Abdul Kallon of the Northern District had, in recent weeks, handed down several decisions regarding several statutory voting safeguards pertaining to absentee ballots. One of the rulings also sought to allow curbside voting. A three-judge appellate panel subsequently overturned these rulings in two separate 2-1 decisions. Read more.

MADISON COUNTY

  1. The current housing shortage across Madison County could continue for the next 3 to 5 years. With organizations such as Toyota, Facebook, and the FBI relocating parts of their operations to the area, it is anticipated that up to 50,000 people will be moving into Madison County over the next 4 years. As a result, the demand for homes, both new and used, is at an unprecedented level. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. HSV PD is investigating the vandalization of 3 churches on Weatherly Road that occurred over the weekend. The suspect was captured on CCTV footage on Saturday night, breaking into and vandalizing the Weatherly Heights Church of Christ on Weatherly Road in south HSV. The same individual is being sought in connection with two other churches' vandalization on the same night. Read more.

    More: If you recognize the individual from the footage, you can call the HSV PD at (256)-883-3791.

  2. Hotels in HSV and the surrounding area are seeing significant drops in revenue as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic. Local officials have noted a 20% decrease in lodging tax for the year, compared with 2019. Such a large drop in income will have a ripple effect as the taxes support other local organizations, including The Von Braun Center, the Huntsville/Madison County Convention & Visitors’ Bureau, and the HSV Sports Commission. Read more.

  3. Toyota is looking to hire 150 new workers at the engine plant located at the North HSV Industrial Park. Positions are available for both production and skill-based maintenance jobs. The positions are part of the plant’s $830M expansion project, ultimately calling for 1,800 total employees by the end of next year. Read more.

    More: Interested candidates can submit applications online for positions in production and skilled maintenance.

MADISON

  1. The Madison City Council has passed a $42 million budget. The cancellation of the Trash Pandas’ inaugural season due to the coronavirus pandemic, and the resultant loss of revenue to the City, has led to a $4M decrease in the budget compared to last year. Regardless, the budget still includes an increase in spending for the Madison City Police Department and a significant investment in local infrastructure and construction projects. City employees will also receive a 3% pay raise. Read more.

Spotlight

May 2020 saw the first crewed space launch from American soil since 2011. It was a thrilling event that marked the culmination of years of development work and an innovative partnership between NASA and private enterprise. It was also the first step on a journey to put humans back on the Moon by 2024 as part of NASA’s Artemis program.

If you would like to explore the complexities of lunar landings and learn about NASA’s plans to establish a sustainable human presence on the Moon, the U.S. Space and Rocket Center will be hosting a show at the Intuitive Planetarium on October 16th called “Astronomy for All: Artemis.” Tickets are available here.
