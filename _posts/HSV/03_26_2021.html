NORTH ALABAMA

  1. Proposed AL Senate Bill 105 may hinder the Singing River Trail development, a long-distance greenway connecting multiple north AL cities. The 150-mile trail collaborates between agencies across Limestone, Madison, and Morgan counties that celebrate the region’s natural and cultural history. The Senate bill, if passed, will stop agencies from exercising eminent domain to provide trails for biking or hiking. Read more.

MADISON COUNTY

  1. The multi-faith community organization Faith in Action is calling on the Madison County District Attorney to make changes to the Pre-Trial Intervention Program. The new Diversion with Dignity: Money & Justice campaign seeks to ensure that qualifying, low-income, first-time offenders have access to the court diversion program regardless of their inability to pay. The group argues that payment requirements adversely impact minority populations - those without financial resources are more likely to accept felony convictions than those who can pay the fees. Read more.

  2. Madison County’s local mask ordinance will expire on April 9, when the statewide mandate ends. HSV Mayor Battle announced at Thursday’s City Council meeting after consultation with the President of the Medical Society Board and the Madison County Department of Public Health. Private and public entities will still require masks in their facilities, and City Hall will likely continue requiring masks for another month or so until numbers drop further. Read more.
