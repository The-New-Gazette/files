HUNTSVILLE

  1. HSV City Schools approved a $300M budget for the 2021 fiscal year. $233M is slated for operating expenses, which is comparable to last year. With uncertainty surrounding the impact of C-19 on schools and students this year, HCS has set aside $30M in reserves, more than a month and a half of the operating budget. Read more.

    More: Having $30M in reserves positions the district well above the state-mandated amount of $18M, and is a dramatic change from the $5.5M deficit in 2018, which resulted from an accounting error. The full HCS FY2021 budget can be found here.

  2. The demolition of the abandoned Haysland Square strip mall on South Memorial Parkway is getting underway. Previously home to retailers such as Belk and Staples, the area has been vacant for several years. Developers plan to open several new retail outlets at the rebuilt location, anchored by a Publix grocery store. Work has already begun work on 400 single-family homes on the land behind the empty mall. Read more.

  3. A defense technology company, Moog, is opening a regional support center in HSV. Based in New York, Moog specializes in the design and manufacture of advanced motion control panels for aerospace, defense, and similar technology applications. The new center will be located at Cummings Research Park for its proximity to Redstone Arsenal and the Marshall Space Flight Center, which the company stated was crucial to its long-term growth strategy. Read more.

    More: Checkout Moog’s career opportunities here.

  4. UAH has set a sixth consecutive record for enrollment, reaching 10k students this year. Enrollment has jumped 36% since 2014 while the school has grown into a nationally recognized university on many fronts, including high research activity and strong economic outcomes for graduates. Read more.

Spotlight

Festifall returns to the HSV Botanical Garden!

Renowned for its elaborate pumpkin creations, chrysanthemum displays, and crisp autumnal colors, the garden continues its celebration of Fall this year with several events for the whole family:

  • The Scarecrow Trail runs until October 5, with two spooky night-time hikes on September 25.
  • The popular ‘Family Campouts in the Garden’ will return September 18-19 and October 2-3.

The garden will also be hosting a Fall Break Camp for grades K-6 from October 5 - 9, providing a fantastic opportunity for children to connect with nature. With each day having a theme and activities, you can sign your child up either for the whole week or individual days.
