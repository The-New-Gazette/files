MADISON CO.

  1. County leaders say local COVID-19 stats continue to improve. Nearly 50 patients have been discharged this week, and test rates have dropped down to 6% positive results from 12% a few weeks ago. Fewer people are also getting tested. Read more.

    Related: HSV Hospital is planning to resume elective surgeries next week, and officials urge people to keep up with routine health care and do not delay visiting the doctor or facilities when necessary. Watch.

  2. The Madison Co. school district has equipped 90 school buses with wi-fi and is parking them in strategic locations throughout the county. Students who have trouble with home internet connections can access the internet from the parking lot around the bus to do assignments. Read more.

    More: Download the MCSS wi-fi Bus Guide

HUNTSVILLE

  1. HSV City Schools opened the 2020 football season yesterday with new safety measures in place. Online ticket purchases are encouraged, and each team is limited to 2000 fans. Players and staff will be tested before games, and fans must wear masks. Concession items will be limited to prepackaged items. Read more.

    More: Check out the Gameday checklist and Area team updates.

  2. CBL Properties, the owners of Parkway Place Mall and 26 other retail properties, is filing for bankruptcy. The company will seek voluntary Chapter 11 protection in October and expects the mall will remain open throughout the restructuring. Visitors should not notice any changes. Read more.

  3. Teledyne Brown Engineering has been awarded a new $29M contract from Raytheon to manufacture Cooling Equipment Units (CEU) in HSV. The CEUs are for the world’s most powerful ground mobile radar system, the Army-Navy/Transportable Radar Surveillance, which is a critical element in the Ballistic Missile Defense System. Read more.

  4. The Land Trust of North Alabama is seeking donations to purchase and preserve a 122-acre tract of land on the northeast corner of Monte Sano mountain. A donor will match donations up to $20,000 from now through Sept. 18. The trust has until February to raise $90,530. Read more.
