News

NORTH ALABAMA

  1. The first week of Sheriff Blakely’s trial ended with wild testimonies around the defendant's business dealings. A business associated with the Sheriff, HIGO LLC, was described as a gun shop, firing range, and a potential conduit for selling weapons and submarines to Taiwan’s federal government. Reports of paying inmates $10/hr to work for the company were also mentioned by witnesses. The case has had such unexpected turns that the State prosecutors have asked for time to ensure records are in order so that charges aren’t dismissed. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. William Darby has resigned from the HSV PD after being convicted of the murder of Jeffrey Parker almost three months ago. He had been on the payroll since his conviction but is no longer a city employee as of Friday. His sentence is scheduled for August. Read more.

  2. A group was escorted out of the HSV City Council meeting Thursday night, and one man was arrested for disorderly conduct. The group was protesting body camera footage laws. Later that night, the council passed a resolution to allow people to view the footage if they have a legitimate need. Read more.

Spotlight

A Huntsville retirement community called Fleming Farms will participate in its own version of the Olympics in honor of the Olympic Games in Tokyo. Several retirement communities across the country are competing with each other remotely. Games will include golf putting, Simon Says, and a game that counts how many sit-to-stands competitors can do in thirty seconds. Their goal is to promote activity for these residents, especially since sedentary lifestyles have become common during quarantine. Read more.
