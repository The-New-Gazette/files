IMPORTANT

  1. Hundreds marched in downtown HSV last night to mark one year after the killing of George Floyd at the hands of Minneapolis police. The mostly silent march down Church St. took 9 minutes and 29 seconds, the time Derek Chauvin pressed his knee onto Floyd’s neck. Before the march, participants watched speakers, musicians, and local religious leaders, who urged the community to help remove race as a factor from police work by increasing civic engagement. Read more.

NORTH ALABAMA

  1. A judge has denied a motion for pretrial release of Falkville resident Lonnie Coffman, who was arrested near the U.S. Capitol riots on January 6. Coffman’s truck was filled with weapons, including multiple guns, hundreds of rounds of ammunition, and Molotov cocktails. A later search of Coffman’s home in Falkville yielded more weapons and ammunition and lists of names of people whose home addresses Coffman intended to find. Read more.

    More: Federal agents discovered Coffman drove past the DC residence of Senator Ted Cruz in 2020 and attempted to contact him; this impacted the Judge’s decision to keep Coffman behind bars. Read more.

MADISON COUNTY

  1. ALDOT has begun the resurfacing of more than 8 miles of road on I-565, with work taking place from 6 pm - 6 am on Sundays -Thursdays. The $16.2 million project, extending from the east of the Intergraph Way underpass to east of the Triana Boulevard overpass, is expected to take about a year to complete. Read more.

  1. The University of Alabama System (HSV, Birmingham, Tuscaloosa) announced that vaccinated students and faculty no longer need to wear masks in most situations. Chancellor Finis St. John stated he is “confident we will have a full, normal, on campus experience” for the Fall 2021 semester. Read more.

CITY OF MADISON

  1. Madison Mayor Finley and the Rocket City Trash Pandas finalized a new payment plan for the $1M the team owes the city. Due to the pandemic-related cancellation of the 2020 baseball season, the city waived $377K and will pay 25% of utilities. BallCorps, the entity behind the Trash Pandas, will pay the remainder due in four payments over the next four months. Read more.
