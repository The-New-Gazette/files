HUNTSVILLE

  1. The HSV City School Board has proposed a bonus for teachers and staff for their hard work in response to the ransomware attack. The $500 supplemental pay package will be voted on Dec 17th as part of a larger $1.4M package. The district was forced to shift to all-paper for the last two weeks but announced on Wed that the primary systems housing student information was not compromised. Read more.

    More: Read the latest cybersecurity update from Superintendent Finley here.

  2. North AL’s first Universal Playground is now open at the new Mark Russell Recreation Center in Hampton Cove. The inclusive design provides a place for people of all abilities to play together. The playground includes wheelchair-accessible flooring, a rock wall, sensory items, and places to retreat. Read more.

  3. Drake State will become a community center for coding and creativity through a partnership with Apple and Tennessee State University (TSU). TSU’s community STEM initiative, combined with Apple’s tools and curriculum for learning the Swift coding language, will focus on enhancing professional development and career advancement opportunities for many in the community. Read more.

    Extra: Here’s an article from Apple’s blog, giving some context to the program when it was run as educator coding academies. Read more.
