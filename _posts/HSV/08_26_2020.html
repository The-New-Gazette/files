NORTH AL

  1. DURA Automotive Systems announced a new facility in Muscle Shoals to manufacture battery trays for electric vehicles. DURA’s $59M investment will create 279 jobs for the local area. This investment is part of an ongoing partnership between DURA and the Shoals Economic Development Authority that includes $14.5M to construct and build-out the facility’s interior. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. New roadworks in downtown HSV are set to continue until summer 2021. Construction has begun on the Jefferson Street Streetscape project, between Holmes Avenue and Spring Street. The project includes improved roadway alignment, decorative sidewalks, streetlamps, landscaping, and irrigation. Access to local businesses will remain open during the construction. Read more.

    Context: Check out downtown HSV Master Plan from Big Picture Huntsville here.

  2. Virtual learning faces the second week of technology problems. On Monday, power outages across the city caused the school district’s WiFi to crash and prevented student and educators access. Schoology, the school district’s learning management system, also went down. School administrators remain upbeat as some parents are beginning to voice their misgivings. Read more

MADISON

  1. Parents will be able to track Madison City School Buses when in-person learning resumes. Madison City Schools has completed the installation of GPS trackers on all school buses, to allow parents to receive alerts once their child’s bus is within an identified zone. A parent portal app will be used to track the buses, and parents will be able to obtain the registration code for their child’s bus directly from the school. Read more.
