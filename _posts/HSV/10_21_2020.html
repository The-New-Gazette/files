IN MEMORIAM

  1. Eula Battle, the wife of HSV Mayor, Tommy Battle, has died at 65. Mrs. Battle had been fighting cancer for some time. An HSV native, she was involved in a wide range of local organizations during her lifetime. She was widely known for her 31 years of teaching in local schools. Mrs. Battle was famous for her love of children and her commitment to education; following her retirement, she continued to teach part-time and set up the non-profit Free 2 Teach that provides free school supplies to Madison County teachers. Read more about her life here.

    More: Instead of flowers, memorials may be sent to Free 2 Teach or Trinity United Methodist Church.

MADISON COUNTY

  1. Jury trials have resumed in Madison County after a hiatus of several months due to C-19. With social-distancing requirements in place going forward, jury pools will be smaller, and masks will be required in the courthouse. As there is a significant backlog of cases to be tried, criminal cases with defendants in jail will be tried first, followed by criminal cases with defendants on bail, with civil trials to be resumed last. Read more.

    More: If you are called for jury duty and have a health concern, you can call the court clerk’s office at (256)-532-3641.

  2. Lockheed Martin has donated $75,000 to school districts across Madison County. The money will be divided equally between the school systems in HSV, Madison City, and Madison County and is intended to support the schools’ technology needs as more focus is placed on virtual learning. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. UAH student group calls for the release of the video footage from a recent controversial traffic stop carried out by campus police. The group, Chargers for Change, is lobbying the UAH President, Darren Dawson, for a series of corrective actions in response to the officers' inappropriate behavior during a routine traffic stop on Oct 3rd. Their demands include the termination of the officers involved in the incident. Read more.

  2. Alabama A&M University has received a $3M grant from the U.S. Department of Defense to support STEM education. The money will be used to establish educational programs focused on the DoD’s science and technology priority areas, to prepare students for technical careers in national security and defense. Alabama A&M is one of twelve schools selected for this grant. Read more.
