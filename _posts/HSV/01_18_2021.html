MADISON COUNTY

  1. Madison County gave its final defense against the state's lawsuit filed for moving the Confederate monument from the courthouse. The state says the removal violated the Alabama Memorial Preservation Act of 2017. The Madison Co. Commission chose to fight the suit, stating that their request to move the statue received tacit approval when the request was not responded to within 90 days. They have challenged the $25k fine and contested provisions in the Alabama Memorial Preservation Act as unconstitutional.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. The soft opening of the C-19 vaccine distribution center at John Hunt Park begins today. About 300 people will be vaccinated today at the Jaycee Community Building, and eventually, the center will distribute 800-900 vaccines each day. 11,000 people have already signed up to receive the vaccine. Watch.

    Action: The waitlist is open for people 75 and older during Phase1B.

    More: Dr. Ali Hassoun, one of the leading doctors at Huntsville Hospital for C-19, answered many questions about the vaccine in a recent interview.

  2. Four UAH scientists co-author groundbreaking research of stellar outbursts from distant stars. These outbursts, called a magnetar, are massive explosions of light and energy from distant neutron stars. “This is the first time we could claim, without a doubt, a giant flare from outside of our galactic neighborhood,” says co-author Dr. Peter Veres, a scientist at UAH’s Center for Space Plasma and Aeronomic Research. Read more.

    More: You can find the research paper in the Nature journal and watch the outburst here.

REDSTONE ARSENAL

  1. NASA’s SLS rocket underwent a core stage test, which was cut short on Saturday. The hot fire is the final test in the Green Run series. All four of the rocket’s RS-45 engines were slated to burn for 8 minutes; however, they automatically shut down after a minute into the test. NASA teams are still working to determine the cause of the early shutdown. While the test is receiving some criticism as a failure of the SLS program, officials insist testing, including unexpected results, is a meaningful part of the process. Read more.
