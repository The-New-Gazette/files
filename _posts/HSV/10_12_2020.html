IMPORTANT

  1. HSV City Schools will commence Mon-Fri in-person classes on Oct. 26 for students who are not enrolled in virtual learning. Public health officials and parent/teacher/student feedback helped inform the decision to re-open schools. A regular disinfecting routine will be implemented, and everyone must wear face coverings. Read more.

  2. Multiple warning sirens in Madison Co. are non-functional as they await repairs, including one on Redstone Arsenal. Residents and workers on the arsenal and around the Celtic Drive siren area in Madison should temporarily find new means to receive outdoor emergency alerts. Repairs are currently scheduled for next week. Read more.

    More: In the meantime, you can keep up with relevant alerts by following Madison County EMA, NWS Huntsville, on Twitter, and alerts for HSV here, and Madison County here.

MADISON COUNTY

  1. The Madison Co. Sherriff’s Office opened a murder investigation into an inmate's death after an altercation with fellow inmates at the county jail Friday morning. Correctional officers called paramedics and administered CPR. The inmate was pronounced dead at HSV Hospital on Friday evening. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. Protesters marched through downtown HSV Friday night to bring attention to the 2019 death of army veteran Crystal Ragland at the hands of HSV Police. Officers met with the protesters beforehand to establish safety guidelines for people on the route. The Ragland family is expected to file a civil rights lawsuit in the coming months. Read more.

QUICK BITS

  • Based on the Madison clinic's success, a new Carl T. Jones location of Urgent Care for Children has opened in HSV. The clinic’s hours are 2 pm - 10 pm on weekdays, and 8 am to 6 pm on weekends. COVID-19 testing is offered. Read more.
  • The HSV-Madison Co. Visitor Center has temporarily closed after an employee tested positive for COVID-19. It will re-open on Wednesday, October 14. In the meantime, visitors should go to the HSV COVID-19 Travel Resource Web page for local facility COVID-19 hours and closures. Read more.
  • Outdoor visits have resumed at the Tut Fann State Veteran’s Home. The facility announced zero COVID-19 cases among staff and residents, down from 50 at its peak. Read more.
  • Registration is currently underway for the Human Exploration Rover Challenge! High school, college, and university students compete to design and test a rover for moon and planet topography each year. Read more.
  • Bob Jones High School counselor Dr. Stephanie Bostick received the 2020 Yale Educator Award, honoring educators who have inspired students to make and achieve academic goals. Dr. Bostick was nominated by former student and current Yale attendee Nayeli Gonzalez-Vazquez. Read more.
Spotlight

If you missed the El Cazador Taco Bus along Governor’s Drive, you aren’t the only one! The bus is back in business after El Cazador moved to a permanent brick-and-mortar location at Stovehouse last year. They now serve food at both locations.

The bus is located at the corner of Triana and Governor’s Drive, a few blocks from its original spot. Bus dine-in is currently closed in adherence with COVID-19 safety measures, but the bus is open for pick-up! Read more.
