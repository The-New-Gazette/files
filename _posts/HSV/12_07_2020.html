NORTH ALABAMA

  1. HSV Hospital Health System will be one of AL's first hospitals to receive C-19 vaccines after FDA approval. The first round, including 6,825 doses of the Pfizer vaccine, will be given to the system’s front-line healthcare workers. Read the full statement here.

    More: Meanwhile, HSV Hospital CEO David Spillers said that while they have more facilities to convert to new wards to handle the rise in C-19 cases, their primary issue is staffing. 235 personnel were out due to contact with C-19 this week. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. A carjacking on Hwy 72 resulted in a police chase Thursday night. Police pursued the man to Five Points, where he crashed into another car. The man accused, Christian Coleman, has been charged with first-degree robbery and is in the hospital with serious injuries. Read more.

  2. Students return to HSV City Schools today and remain temporarily unplugged following a cyber-attack two weeks ago. School leaders ask that all district-issued devices be turned off and that all students, even those learning virtually, use pen and paper until the investigation ends. School printers are working overtime to produce paper lessons and associated resources for students. Read more.

  3. Local HSV aerospace startup Aevum unveiled what it billed as the world’s largest autonomous drone (AULV). The Ravn X is intended to bring some competition into the small launch market by delivering communication satellites into orbit at prices lower than the conventional delivery by a rocket. The first launch using the 80 foot long autonomous aircraft will be in support of the U.S. Air Force. Read more.

Spotlight

HudsonAlpha has published its annual biotech guidebook to keep science educators updated on the past year's most important discoveries. After reviewing hundreds of studies from the past twelve months, the Educational Outreach team selects those most relevant to high school curriculums. It then presents them in a way that is engaging and accessible for students.

This edition highlights over four dozen findings in agriculture, human health, and other bioscience fields. The digital version is free to the public here.
