NORTH ALABAMA

  1. The USDA is funding 6 new projects across AL through the Distance Learning and Telemedicine (DLT) grant program. Central North AL Health Services has been awarded $173,818, and the Madison Co. Board of Education has been awarded $775,058. Program funds will help buy equipment and software needed to provide telemedicine and remote-learning services for rural residents. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. UAH and Calhoun Community College are partnering to offer a Dual Nursing Program starting in Spring 2021. The collaboration allows students to pursue an Associate Degree in Nursing on the Calhoun Campus and a Bachelor of Science in Nursing online at UAH in only five semesters. Read more.

  2. Despite continued reduced traffic, HSV International Airport continues to avoid layoffs with budget reductions and stimulus funds. The airport is currently seeing 30 percent of its normal traffic, up from only 5 percent earlier this year. Flight, hotel, and car bookings continue to stagnate. Read more.

  3. C-Store Master broke ground this week on a new $12M automated warehouse in HSV. The state of the art distribution warehouse, which is the first of its kind in the Southeast, will include three new robotic technologies from logistics partner GEEK+. The facility is set to open in Q1 next year and will create 40 new jobs. Read more.

  4. The HSV Police Department has a new Public Information Officer. Lt. Jesse Sumlin, a retired U.S. Army veteran and 25-year employee of HPD, will serve as a liaison between HPD, media, and citizens. Read more.

CITY OF MADISON

  1. Connie Spears won Tuesday’s runoff election for Madison City Council District 2. The former Board of Education member defeated incumbent Steve Smith by 184 votes. She is the third woman elected to the Madison City Council this year and will begin her four-year term on Nov. 1. She ran on a platform of smart growth, fiscal responsibility, and transparency. Read more.
