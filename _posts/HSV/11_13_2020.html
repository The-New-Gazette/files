NORTH ALABAMA

  1. AL set to receive $25 billion for projects and programs next year if the latest budget gets approved. U.S. Senator Richard Shelby (R-AL), chairman of the Senate Appropriations Committee, released twelve Fiscal Year 2021 federal spending bills Wednesday. The proposed budget includes $10.8 billion for the Army Missile Defense Agency, $568 million for hypersonics research at Redstone Arsenal, $2.6 billion for the Space Launch System, and $566 million for FBI construction in HSV. Read more.

  2. HSV Hospital is preparing for potential storage and distribution of the C-19 vaccine in North AL. The hospital has enough cold storage capacity for roughly 30,000 doses at a time. The AL Department of Public Health outlines that the vaccine will first be available for health care workers and then the most medically vulnerable populations. Read more.

  3. The Land Trust of North Alabama and Cane Creek Canyon Nature Preserve have entered into a partnership to protect 693 acres in Northwest AL. The Cane Creek Canyon area in Colbert Co. hosts rock shelters, waterfalls, significant biodiversity, and 15 miles of public hiking trail. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. UAH released an update Wednesday on efforts to ensure the long-term viability of the Chargers hockey program. Plans include a proposed multi-purpose facility for the team on the UAH campus and a push for the team to join a new conference. Read more.

    Context: The Charger program was almost eliminated back in May due to budget concerns. With the help of alumni and Minnesota Wild goalie Cam Talbot, supporters raised $17 million in pledges to provide financial support for the program over the next 10 years. Read more.
