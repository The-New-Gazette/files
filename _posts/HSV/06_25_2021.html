NORTH ALABAMA

  1. Local nonprofit organization Tennessee Riverkeeper (TR) plans to sue the Guntersville Water and Sewage Board for allowing large amounts of sewage into the nearby Tennessee River. TR founder David Whiteside claims that the group must enforce the Clean Water Act because the Alabama Department of Environmental Management has not done its job. The Guntersville Water and Sewage Board supports TR’s efforts and plans to cooperate in the lawsuit, claiming to have previously raised the issue with the state. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. Two people have been charged with the abuse of a corpse after the body of a woman was discovered Thursday morning in Five Points. Police believe the man and woman hid the body of Laekyn Steelman, an acquaintance, who overdosed the previous evening. Madison County Coroner Tyler Berryhill expects an autopsy this week to be performed by the state forensic lab. Read more.

  2. On Thursday, a man was struck by a car and killed on Mastin Lake Road, marking HSV’s third traffic fatality this week. While police are investigating the collision, the driver remained on the scene, and no charges are expected to be filed. Read more.
