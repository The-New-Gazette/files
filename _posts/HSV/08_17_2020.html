IMPORTANT

  1. Dr. Jian Han of HudsonAlpha said that a C-19 vaccine or treatment could be ready this fall or winter during a virtual benefit. He and his international team are also researching a new test method utilizing a patient’s cough, which could replace the current painful swab test. Results could be scanned into a bar code, allowing those who test negative to work and travel freely. Dr. Han also headed the team that developed a C-19 test currently being used 10k times per day. HUNTSVILLE

  1. The HSV City Council has requested that Gov. Ivey call a special session of the legislature to address the Madison County Confederate memorial. This request attempts to speed up the timeline for the assembly to re-consider the 2017 law protecting the statue. Meanwhile, City Council President Devyn Keith has pledged to ask the Madison County Commission to give the statue to the City of HSV so that it may be relocated. Read more.

    More: Thursday evening saw demonstrators gather for another protest of the memorial. Reads more.

  2. South Memorial Parkway development The Market at Hays Farm has announced Publix Supermarket as its anchor store. The development will join the finished South Parkway Restore Our Roads project, new Grissom High School, and expanded greenway trails as part of an updated south HSV landscape. The center is slated to open in fall 2021. Read more.

    More: Explore the Hays Farm history and master plan.

  3. The new Johnson Legacy Center in north HSV is now open after a 5.3M renovation. The facility will be managed and staffed by the Parks & Rec department and run on a membership model. Amenities include a weight room, futsal/volleyball courts, an indoor rock climbing wall, and more. The re-purposed high school is the first phase of a larger planned community project which includes the community center as the hub, commercial space, various housing types, and greenways. Read more.

    More: Fitness memberships are $15 per month and $30 per month for additional access to the climbing wall. See the fees
