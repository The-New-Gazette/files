NORTH ALABAMA

  1. Former US Ambassador to Slovenia and Auburn graduate Lynda Blanchard announced her candidacy for Richard Shelby’s Senate seat. Blanchard was appointed ambassador by former President Trump in 2018 and has closely aligned herself with him and his supporters. She also announced she would be making a $5M deposit to her campaign account to compete for the Republican nomination. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. Vaccination efforts in HSV have gone well for those 75 and older but need to ramp up for certain at-risk groups. 45% of those over age 75 have received vaccines, while lower age brackets have yet to crest 20%. City leaders also emphasized the importance of vaccinating members of the Black community who have been disproportionately affected by C-19. Only 11% of AL’s vaccines have reached the Black community so far. Watch more.

    More: At Wednesday’s Madison Co C-19 briefing, Dr. Warren Strickland from Crestwood Hospital emphasized that leaders and health-care providers must make a concerted effort to reach out to the Black community with education and access to the vaccine. Read more.

  2. A national police-reform advocacy group will campaign for HSV law enforcement reforms after constituent concerns continue over police violence at summer protests. Activists are pushing for increased departmental transparency, new rules for the use of tear gas, and increased mental health and de-escalation training, as well as alternatives to policing for some situations. Read more.

    Context: The Grassroots Law Project utilizes volunteers in cities with large minority populations to push for policy changes to improve public safety and save police time and resources.

  3. The University of Alabama in HSV will receive a $250K bequest to ensure the UAH Hockey program's longevity. The Schonrock family announced the gift Wednesday, left by the late Elizabeth “Betty” Schonrock. After years of work at IBM, Mrs. Schonrock volunteered with several HSV organizations, including the Ice Complex, the HSV Museum of Art, and the U.S Space & Rocket Center. Read more.

  4. Raytheon challenges Boeing’s $4.4B acquisition of Aerojet Rocketdyne with the U.S. departments of Defense and Justice. Raytheon expresses concerns that the deal would stifle competition and allow a top competitor to absorb a key supplier of solid-fuel rocket motors used in Raytheon’s missile systems. All three parties have a presence in HSV. Read more.
