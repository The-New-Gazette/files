HUNTSVILLE

  1. Acting Air Force Secretary John Roth confirmed that no political forces influenced the decision to move Space Command headquarters to HSV. In a Congressional hearing on Wednesday, Roth testified that, despite recent upgrades to the current facilities in Colorado, Alabama’s lower construction and maintenance costs led the Air Force to choose HSV. Read more.

  2. In a proposal to bring commercial space operations to HSV, the HSV International Airport and Sierra Space have officially submitted applications for operations. The HSV IA seeks a site re-entry operator license, and Sierra Space requests a vehicle operator license to land its Dream Chaser after payload-carrying missions. The FAA will consider safety and environmental impacts before deciding whether or not to issue the licenses. Read more.

Spotlight

News of the Emancipation Proclamation did not reach many enslaved people living in the south until months after its official announcement on January 1, 1863. June 19, or Juneteenth, is the day we celebrate the end of American enslavement. No public events were held in 2020 in HSV due to C-19, so 2021’s celebration will be significant given that it has also become an official Federal holiday this week. Join one of the many events happening this weekend around the Rocket City, including a VBC market, a can’t-be-missed opera performance at Campus 805’s SoulFest, and an extensive lineup of musicians at The Camp.
