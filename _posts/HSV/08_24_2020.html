IMPORTANT

  1. Gov. Ivey issued a supplement to an emergency proclamation granting municipalities leeway in enlisting and training poll workers. The League of Municipalities reported difficulties recruiting poll workers because of C-19. Election workers may now live in the county where the election is located (rather than strictly within the city limits), and they can be trained via telephone. These measures will affect the upcoming August 25 and October 6 elections only. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. HSV Chamber is addressing families’ child care concerns during C-19. Over half of surveyed working parents in Mad. Co. reported dire unmet child care needs as schools open online only for the first few weeks. The HSV-MadCo Chamber of Commerce Child Care Task Force is currently working to secure the required $50K to launch a grant program to cover applicants’ child care costs. Read more.

MADISON CO.

  1. The Mad. Co. Sheriff’s Office has certified a team of mental health officers to handle incidents where mental health is a factor. These officers, who have received 200 hours of training with mental health professionals, will work to de-escalate heightened situations and divert people with mental health issues directly to the hospital, if needed, instead of jail. Read more.

REDSTONE ARSENAL

  1. The Army Space and Missile Defense Command (ASMDC) has re-aligned under the leadership of Space Command (USSPACECOM). The ASMDC previously reported to U.S. Strategic Command, and the shift to USSPACECOM will be a closer alignment as they continue to handle a variety of space-related operations, including satellites, communications, and providing astronauts. The HQ will remain split between Redstone Arsenal, AL, and Colorado Springs, CO. Read more.

    More: To be clear, the U.S. has a Space Force and a Space Command, and they’re still working out lines of responsibility with space operations. Read more.
