A Good Ending.

Good morning dear readers!

While this past year was a perfect time to experiment with streamlining the delivery of relevant local news, we have determined that the service is not viable over the long term at this time. It has been our sincere pleasure to curate the local news and provide an easier way to be informed and engaged with our growing city. Thank you for joining us on this journey these many months.

All subscriptions will be suspended with no more charges after today. To honor our commitment to you, we will provide our service through the end of next month. Our last edition will be Friday, July 30th.

We wish you all the very best and hope you continue to engage with the news wisely and live well in HSV.

Kindest Regards,

The New Gazette Team

HUNTSVILLE

  1. 106 Jefferson, a new downtown HSV hotel, part of the Curio Collection by Hilton, is expected to open later this summer. The luxury hotel will feature Baker & Able, a rooftop bar named in honor of two intrepid space monkeys, Revivalist, a full-service restaurant serving revived European classics and flexible event space. Bookings are available now for late this summer, with rooms starting at around $200. Read more.

  • Fireworks and Watermelon Carving contest at MidCity District
  • Fourth of July Fireworks Spectacular at Toyota Field
  • Madison’s Star Spangled Celebration
  • Celebrate July 4th with the Town of Gurley
  • New Market Family Freedom Fest

  1. A groundbreaking ceremony was held for the new $1.3B FBI Innovation Center at Redstone Arsenal. The campus will focus on intelligence, cyber threats, and analytics, house around 4-5 thousand jobs, and include a kinetic cyber range and VR classrooms for agent training simulations. Construction on the initial phase of the 9 building campus is expected to be complete in 2023. Read more.

Spotlight

The National Parks Service designated Edmonton Heights as a historic district listed in the National Register of Historic Places. Edmonton Heights, located northeast of downtown HSV, adjacent to AAMU, is the most well-preserved African American neighborhood in Alabama established by Folmar and Finn, one of the largest Southern speculative building companies in the South in the post World War II era.
