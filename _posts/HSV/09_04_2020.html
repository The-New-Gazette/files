IMPORTANT

  1. There will be six amendments to the AL state constitution on the ballot this November. Amendment 1 provides that only a U.S. citizen has the right to vote. Amendments 2 and 3 relate to changes to the state court system. Amendment 4 would reorganize and update language in the state constitution. Amendments 5 and 6 are county-specific to Franklin and Lauderdale counties. Read more.

MADISON CO.

  1. Construction is behind schedule, and costs have increased to build the new Madison Co. Services Center. Security screening equipment and window coverings have increased costs by $122,000, bringing the project’s price tag to over $11 million. Meanwhile, rain delays have crews two weeks behind schedule. Read more.

    Context: Construction began late last year on a new center to move several services away from the downtown courthouse to an area with free customer parking and room for growth. Service Center Highlights.

  2. Local health officials urge people to exercise caution this Labor Day weekend. Previous holiday weekends have been followed by spikes in C-19 cases. Current case numbers may not be as low as reported due to changes in testing and reporting methods. With schools reopening and flu season approaching, officials say it is vital to continue mask-wearing, hand washing, and social distancing. Flu vaccines are recommended for those over six months of age. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. Three more residents of the Tut Fann Veteran Home have died since Friday, bringing the death toll to 10 in less than three weeks. Seven of those ten residents had previously tested positive for C-19. The facility is awaiting confirmation from the AL Department of Public Health before announcing the cause of death for any residents. Two previously announced employee deaths are still under investigation, with one employee testing positive for C-19. Read more.
