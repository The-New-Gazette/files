HUNTSVILLE

  1. HSV City Clerk-Treasurer Ken Benion announced the city has begun counting provisional ballots to discover if there is a clear winner in the District 5 council race. Once the Madison Co. Board of Registrars validates the provisional ballots, the city council will count the votes in a special session on Monday. If a runoff election is still needed, it will take place on October 6. Read more.

    Correction: TNG reported on Wednesday that Will Culver was re-elected with 50% of the vote. Culver received 49.6% of the vote, followed by John Meredith with 39.93% and Tom Hopf with 12.42%. No candidate reached the 50% benchmark to claim a clear win.

  2. MartinFederal Consulting has been awarded part of a $7.5 billion Department of Defense subcontract. The HSV-based, veteran-owned company will provide engineering expertise for DoD information technology systems and solutions through support of the prime contractor, Integrated Systems, Inc. Read more.

  3. The U.S. Space & Rocket Center is offering a new day camp for 1st - 6th-grade students participating in virtual learning. The Community Day Camp provides time for schoolwork in addition to enrichment activities and physical exercise. Camp starts on September 7 and costs $249 per week. Read more.

  4. HSV Utilities, the U.S. Space & Rocket Center, and Seven States Power have partnered for Project Liftoff, a new power flexibility exhibit scheduled for completion in September. The project will integrate three technologies to offset energy usage and serve visitors. A solar canopy, battery station, and two Level 2 electric vehicle chargers will be located in the main guest parking area near the space center’s entrance. Read more.

  5. HSV City Council has passed a resolution authorizing legal actions to protect the city’s interests in the Remington Arms bankruptcy case. The company previously agreed to property investments and job creation in exchange for local incentives but has since filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy. The city is in communication with the bankruptcy court and with potential bidders to help ensure the sale of the facility protects remaining jobs and payment of the full mortgage debt. Read more.
