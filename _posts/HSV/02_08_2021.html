MADISON COUNTY

  1. Some HSV groups are pushing for statewide legalization of recreational marijuana as an updated version of last year’s Compassion Act bill is considered. The previous bill which proposed the legalization of medical marijuana was passed and then delayed due to C-19. The local groups are encouraging the broader legalization in the revised bill citing racial disparities in the policing of marijuana possession in Madison County. Read more.

    Context: The claims of disparity in policing are based on a study from the Alabama Appleseed Center for Law and Justice advocacy group. You can learn more about the study here.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. A shooting occurred around 12:30 am Saturday on Regia Drive. The victim, Jamie Howard, was killed, and Russell Howard Jr. is in custody after turning himself in peacefully at the crime scene. The shooting is still under investigation, and a motive hasn’t been declared. Read more.

  2. The AL Court of Criminal Appeals upheld Stephen Marc Stone's conviction for the 2013 murders of his wife and son in South Huntsville. The appeal to overturn the conviction was on the grounds that some evidence should have been excluded from the case, claims of misconduct during closing arguments, and objections to some jurors. The core argument that the evidence from the case supported the initial plea of not guilty by reason of insanity was rejected by the appeals court. Read more.

MADISON

  1. A construction project on Zierdt Road has caused dirt runoff to muddy the waters of Lady Ann Lake. A new drainage system is being installed to replace the current one, which was in disrepair, to prevent further runoff and protect the lake. Read more.

  2. The search for a new Madison Police Chief is underway. The city website recently posted the job, stating they seek an “experienced, visionary leader with strong character.” You can see the recruitment brochure here and the job posting here.

Spotlight

Huntsville’s Botanical Garden is now hosting Dog Days daily through Feb. 28, an event that encourages visitors to get out of the house and take their dogs for a walk through the gardens. A day-trip with your favorite four-legged friend only costs $3 or $15 for a season pass. You can purchase tickets here.

If you’re looking to adopt, HSV Animal Services reduced their adoption fee for most large adult dogs until Feb. 13. A $10–$35 fee covers spay/neuter surgery, vaccinations, microchip, heartworm check, a city license, and a bag of dog food. You can see a list of adoptable animals here.
