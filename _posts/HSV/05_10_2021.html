IMPORTANT

  1. On Friday, a jury found Officer William Darby of the HSV PD guilty of the murder of Jeffrey Parker. Darby fatally shot Parker in 2018 after responding to a 911 mental health crisis call. Darby’s attorneys argued that his actions were in defense of his fellow officers, but the prosecutors maintained that Darby escalated the situation. Read more.

NORTH ALABAMA

  1. UPDATE: Most North Alabama universities are planning in-person graduation ceremonies this spring. One exception is UAH, which will have a virtual ceremony. The majority of in-person ceremonies will be held outdoors, following C-19 safety regulations. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. Matsu Alabama will expand production at its HSV facility, including an $8.5 million investment in upgraded equipment. The automotive parts supplier will also add at least ten new full-time jobs to its existing 217-member team and two new Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs) to its client base. This will mark Matsu Alabama’s third local expansion since 2004. Read more.
