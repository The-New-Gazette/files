HUNTSVILLE

  1. All HSV City School students not enrolled in Virtual Academy will return to classrooms five days a week starting Monday, Oct. 26, for the second nine weeks of school. In-person students will continue attending in cohorts using a staggered schedule until this date. Virtual Academy students will continue online learning through the rest of the semester. Read more.

  2. NASA’s ninth annual International Space Apps Challenge is underway this weekend. The 48-hour global hackathon brings people together to solve complex global and space science issues. This year’s theme is “Take Action,” and for the first time, NASA will work with four other space agencies to share datasets with participants and judge solutions. Alabama A&M University is this year’s local host. Read more.

  3. Researchers at UAH are searching for adults to participate in the Inner Defense Initiative. The study is looking into the biological aspects of post-traumatic stress disorder. Volunteers are needed to provide blood samples, cheek swabs, and answers to a survey. Read more.

  4. BIO Alabama is holding an annual virtual conference next week, Oct. 5-9. The event for Alabama bioscience industries will feature HudsonAlpha and several other North AL based companies. Read more.

CITY OF MADISON

  1. The City of Madison budget passed Monday and included $200K for cybersecurity improvements. This amounts to one-fifth of the city’s capital budget slated for IT advancements, including software upgrades and employee training. Cybersecurity concerns have increased for cities as more activities and work take place online. Read more.
