HUNTSVILLE

  1. Protesters marched in downtown HSV on Friday night to express their frustration at the Louisville ruling regarding the death of Breonna Taylor. The demonstrators, who remained peaceful, drew attention to the May 2019 death of Crystal Ragland by HPD and added their voices to a renewed call for HPD Chief Mark MacMurray to step down. Read more.

    More: At Thursday’s city council meeting, where several members of the public voiced concerns about the HPD Chief, HSV mayor Tommy Battle responded that he remains fully confident in both the HSV police force and its Chief. Read more.

  2. UAH has received $3.7M in grant money from the U.S. DoD to establish the Alabama Defense Advanced Manufacturing Community (ADAMC) program. This ADAMC will be spread across three AL universities with UAH focused on workplace training and the introduction of advanced manufacturing technology for modern aviation, missiles, and ground vehicle systems. The training program is expected to have a significant impact in supporting military veteran workers. Read more.

  3. CNI Solutions and Oakwood University are partnering with Chattanooga based CO.STARTERS to open the Community Empowerment Entrepreneur and Education Center. The facility will focus on encouraging and educating women entrepreneurs and work skills to formerly incarcerated persons re-entering the workforce. The center will open in early October and is currently accepting applications for its new programs. Read more.
