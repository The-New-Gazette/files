News

IMPORTANT

  1. C-19 continues to affect the 2020 fall semester for schools in Madison County. In the Madison City School system, 170 students and faculty are currently under quarantine, with Bob Jones High School accounting for the majority of those people, with 130. Read more.

    More: The school district will delay its high school reopening by one week. With nine players testing positive for COVID, the Bob Jones football team canceled their game with Florence this past Friday night and postposed homecoming until Oct. 23. Read more.

    More: Due to a rise in COVID cases and quarantines, Huntsville’s AAA magnet school will switch back to virtual learning beginning today, with plans to reopen the school on Oct. 1. Read more.

  2. Unemployment in Alabama fell to 5.6% in August, a significant decrease from July’s reported rate of 7.9%. These numbers represent the ninth-best recovery in the U.S, with manufacturing and construction reporting record-high earnings. At only 3.3%, unemployment in the city of Madison rated among the lowest in the state. Read more.

NORTH AL

  1. Congress passed the S. 881 PROSWIFT Act last week, which will allow scientists to research the enigmatic processes and effects of space weather. The Marshall Space Flight Center’s Artemis Program, including many North Alabama private sector businesses, could see significant benefits from playing a role in this research and the results it yields. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. The Huntsville Assistance Program, which provides non-perishable food items for those in need, is out of food. More local people are suffering from food insecurity due to the extended circumstances of C-19. While churches have assisted with food, there have been fewer food drives to support the program, and demand has exceeded supply. HAP is hopeful that pantry shelves will be stocked again in October. Read more.

Spotlight

The Huntsville Botanical Garden is once again preparing for the annual Galaxy of Lights! For health and safety purposes, the Garden will be making a few changes in 2020, including walking tours staggered by time, so tickets must be reserved in advance.

Pay a visit to the Botanical Garden’s website to find times and dates for this fun drive-through event!
