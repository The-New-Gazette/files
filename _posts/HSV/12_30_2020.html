IMPORTANT

  1. A man detonated his RV in downtown Nashville on Christmas Day, killing himself, wounding eight others, and causing serious damage to forty nearby buildings. Federal investigators have confirmed his identity as Anthony Quinn Warner, 63, a resident of the Nashville metro area. Investigators are questioning relatives and acquaintances to determine a motive. So far, officials have stated that it was clearly intentional and didn't seem politically motivated. Read more.

NORTH ALABAMA

  1. GOP leaders have rebuffed Senator-elect Tuberville on challenging the presidential election during Congress’s final count on Jan 6. Senator-elect Tuberville and Representative Brooks have mentioned raising an objection during the electoral vote counting process, requiring Congress's joint session to separate and deliberate on whether to approve or deny the objection. Senate Republicans have expressly stated they want to avoid this situation and said it is doubtful the move would impact the election’s outcome. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. A growing number of children with C-19 are becoming inpatients at the HSV Hospital’s Women and Children campus. The available C-19 vaccines are not currently an option for children as pediatric doses of the vaccine have not been approved yet. The Pfizer vaccine is approved for ages 16+ and Moderna for 18+. On a positive note, all HSV Hospitals' pediatric doctors have received the vaccine, with none reacting negatively. Read more.

    Extra: See the latest numbers from HSV Hospital here.

  2. The residents and staff at the Floyd E. ‘Tut’ Fann State Veterans Home are expected to receive the first round of the C-19 Pfizer vaccine on Dec. 31. Health Management Resources of Alabama, INC., the health care provider for the state veterans facilities, has partnered with Walgreens to administer the vaccine. Read more.

MADISON

  1. The Madison City Council passed two residential re-zoning ordinances, which will add around 250 new homes to the city. Several residents opposed the re-zoning arguing that the roads and schools wouldn’t handle the growth and increased congestion. The properties which will be re-zoned are Barnett’s Crossing and Bellawoods West. Read more.

    More: Madison City Schools superintendent Dr. Ed Nichol assured the city council that future city growth would not compromise the district’s high educational standards.

Spotlight

The Tunnel to Towers Foundation paid the mortgage on Billy Clardy III's family home, who was killed in the line of duty in December 2019. Clardy, an Army veteran, had served with the Huntsville Police Department since 2005. Read more.

Ashlee Clardy, his widow, made a statement saying, “It means the world to me and my family to have the mortgage paid off on our home. I am truly speechless. Tunnel To Towers is doing great work not only for our family but for other families that have also suffered the loss of a loved one. Not having to be burdened by a mortgage is a huge relief.”
