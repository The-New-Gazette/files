IMPORTANT

  1. The AL Department of Public Health (ADPH) launched an online portal to check eligibility and make appointments for the C-19 vaccine. ADPH will also be opening eight large drive-through clinics throughout the state, including Huntsville, which will scale to deliver 1,000 doses a day. Read more.

    Context: See the latest numbers from HSV Hospital here and the vaccine distribution dashboard.

  2. Teachers, food workers, people 65 and older, and others in vaccine phase 1b will be eligible to receive the C-19 vaccine on Feb. 8. Due to a shortage of vaccine doses in AL, State Health Officer Dr. Scott Harris has asked that lower-risk people in the eligible group consider waiting to allow higher-risk people to receive the vaccine first. Read more.

    More: Teachers can join the HSV Hospital C-19 vaccine waitlist here.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. The Spencer Companies based in downtown HSV is being sold after almost 100 years of service. The company, which has operated in the gas and convenience store industry since 1923, has sold its operating assets to the Dutch Oil Company of Mississippi. The Spencer Companies were well-known to support the city and have family ties with former Mayor Loretta Spencer. Read more.

  2. HSV School’s Desegregation Advisory Committee (DAC) held its first public meeting of 2021 this week. Since 2015 HSV Schools have been part of a federal desegregation consent order, with DAC monitoring progress across seven troubled areas. A DAC representative affirmed that racial equality had been reached in one of the seven areas, transportation, in 2020, and they are pushing for more improvement in 2021. You can see the list of other planned public hearings here.

    Context: Here’s an article from last year about the consent order and transportation equality in HSV Schools. Read more.

  3. Work has begun on the Constellation mixed-used development off Clinton Ave in downtown HSV. While the project has laid dormant for a long time, with the plot being purchased for re-development in 1961, initial site preparations are underway. The project is touted as a new “front door” for downtown, and plans include an apartment complex, parking garages, retail, and an office building. The office building could range between 13 to 20 floors, potentially making it the tallest building downtown. Read more.

    Context: There's a long history related to the Constellation development, which you can check out here.
