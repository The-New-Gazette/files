NORTH ALABAMA

  1. U.S. Senator-elect Tommy Tuberville announced the members of his transition committee. Stan McDonald, a lawyer from Huntsville, will serve as chairman. McDonald worked closely with Tuberville on the campaign process and served as a campaign co-chairman during the general election. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. At the HSV City Council Meeting on Thursday, citizens pushed for police reform. The family of the late Dana Fletcher, who was shot and killed by Madison police officers a year ago, encouraged the council to sponsor the Dana Fletcher Bill. The bill would set requirements on the storage and viewing access of body camera footage. Read more.

  2. Three HSV high schools return to in-person learning today. Last week, students of Lee, New Century, and Columbia high schools temporarily returned to virtual learning as multiple staff members were in quarantine, leaving the schools understaffed. Read more.

    Context: Some students have expressed their concerns about returning to class. You can find the latest C-19 stats for the school district here.

  3. A HudsonAlpha researcher collaborated on a project sequencing the genome of a possible alternative sustainable fuel source. This study suggests miscanthus grasses may be used as biomass and act as an alternative to petroleum-based fossil fuels. This was the first sequence and genomic analysis of miscanthus and will help future studies optimize its desirable traits and discover the likelihood of practical applications. Read more.

MADISON

  1. Madison County Schools received a $7,500 grant for STEM activities from The Armed Forces Communications Electronics Association. The grant will give students at Madison County High, Sparkman Middle School, and Walnut Grove Elementary better access to technology, robotics, and design programs. Read more.

Spotlight

Drawings for HSV’s new seven-level city hall were presented to the city council on Thursday. The new building will be built just across the street from the current building, diagonal to the Madison County Courthouse. Construction will begin in 2021 and last about 20 months. The project is expected to cost $60M, although the budget has not been approved yet. Read more.
