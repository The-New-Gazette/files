STATEWIDE

  1. Three educators from Limestone County, Tuscaloosa, and Uniontown admitted to reporting false enrollment numbers in order to receive more state funding. They also admitted to using some of these extra payments for personal use. All three stand to serve up to five years in prison. Their trial begins Sept. 13, 2021. Read more.

  2. AL Secretary of State John Merrill is facing backlash for past social media posts and racist remarks, which he denies having said. A legal assistant with whom Merrill formerly had an affair has accused him of using derogatory terms about the Black community throughout their relationship. Merrill formerly denied the affair but then admitted to it. Read more.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. Today HSV Hospital will open a new online portal to make it easier to schedule vaccination appointments. Communication issues involving a third-party telephone operator frequently complicate the appointment scheduling process. The new system will allow patients to choose a convenient date and time. Book an appointment here.

REDSTONE ARSENAL

  1. The Army at Redstone Arsenal has awarded a $3.6B contract to SAIC. The contract is for engineering services, including the testing and training of simulation systems, and will create many new employment opportunities in the HSV area. SAIC’s proposal included 77 smaller companies who will have the opportunity to support this contract. Read more.

  • A shooting on Cypress Point Drive on Saturday resulted in the death of one man. His wife has been booked at the Madison County Jail and charged with murder. Read more.
  • A vigil bringing attention to the handling of mental illness response was held last night in Big Spring Park. The family of Bradley Pugh, the man killed by HSV police in a 2020 standoff, organized the event. Read more.

  • Starting today, AL 36 south of Somerville will be closed for bridge replacement. For a detour, take I65 to AL 67 eastbound (and vice versa westbound). Read more.
  • Cummings Research Park will host three events in the Paralympics Cycling Open April 17–18. Qualifying cyclists will compete in Tokyo in the summer. If you would like to watch these events, find directions, a map, and parking instructions here.
Spotlight

The Cinemagic Theatre, Athens’ only locally owned movie theatre complex, has struggled for business throughout the pandemic and has shut down completely on weekdays. They are hoping to see ticket sales improve during the summer season. Several major titles are coming to their screens soon, including Black Widow, Top Gun, and films from the James Bond franchise.
