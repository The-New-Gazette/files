IMPORTANT

  1. The HSV City School Board suggested that nine weeks of digital education could be reduced. A group known as M.U.S.E. (Municipalities United to Support Education) is investigating whether resuming in-person classes sooner is possible. The HSV School Board emphasized that any such decision would be data-driven and dependent on the community’s compliance with public health edicts. Read more.

NORTH ALABAMA

  1. A mountaintop mansion in Morgan County is being converted into a residential drug and alcohol addiction treatment center. The new facility, called Sereno Ridge Recovery, will provide addiction and detox services in a less-institutionalized setting. The new 16,500 sq ft center is a welcomed addition for the HSV-Decatur-Albertville area, which has only two treatment centers with 72 beds in total. Read more.

    More: Sereno Ridge Recovery will treat around 300 people per year, which brings our area’s capacity to 32% of demand for drug and alcohol addiction treatment.

HUNTSVILLE

  1. HSV will receive a $12.5M federal grant for a new multi-modal transit station downtown. The new facility, located on the site of the old Sherman Concrete plant off Church St. and Pratt Ave., will accommodate city buses, Greyhound coaches, taxis, and ride-share services. The new station is estimated to cost around $19.1M and be open by 2022. Read more.

    More: See renderings of the proposed transit station project here.

  2. Local company KBR, Inc. awarded a $165M supporting munitions program on Redstone Arsenal. The Tactical Aviation and Ground Munitions (TAGM) program will build on previous projects undertaken by KBR, with the work conducted over the next five years on Redstone Arsenal. This specific project will include research and engineering of state-of-the-art tactical projectiles, including the Hellfire missile. Read more.

  3. HSV’s Toyota Engine Plant will receive the largest solar array in the country. The 3.3-acre array will generate an estimated 1.6 megawatts of energy. Using solar power will reduce carbon dioxide emissions from the plant by as much as 1,732 MT each year. The $2.7M project is slated for completion by December 2020. Read more.

MADISON

  1. Savannah Demeester has been named the new principal of West Madison Elementary School (WMES). A former student of WMES, Ms. Demeester holds undergraduate and graduate degrees from UAB in both Education and Education Leadership. She previously taught at Columbia Elementary and was most recently the Assistant Principal at Rainbow Elementary. WMES’ outgoing principal, Dr. Daphne Jah, has moved on to become the personnel coordinator for Madison City Schools. Read more.

Spotlight

There is a new virtual reality arcade coming to town! A local family with roots in rockets and gaming are opening Altered Reality in Madison at 72 and Nance Rd.

The arcade will initially offer 50 games across four VR stations to accommodate social distancing, and eventually expand with more VR stations and an area for tabletop gaming. You will be able to schedule 30-minute or 1-hour time slots on their website at youralteredreality.com.

They plan to launch their website and location within the next seven days — stay tuned!
